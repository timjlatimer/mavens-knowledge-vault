## Phase 1 Findings

### 1. Rigorous Baseline Assessment

The source documents (`maven_ai_team_brief.md` and `maven_italy_reconnaissance_brief.md`) were re-evaluated with formal scoring, calibrated through the lens of the **Ruby Red** North Star. The goal is to assess their fitness as a starting point for the **Maven Local North Star** document.

| Dimension     | Score | North Star-Calibrated Justification                                                                                                                                                              |
|:--------------|:------|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Completeness  | 16/20 | The documents are exceptionally complete regarding the *current state* of the Maven/Bingo City app and its technical architecture. For Ruby Red, this means the foundation is solid. However, they lack a complete vision for the *future state* of the product.                                |
| Clarity       | 16/20 | The briefs are clear for a strategic and technical audience. However, for Ruby Red's needs, the vision isn't yet translated into a simple, emotionally resonant narrative that the entire AI team (especially VERA, PIXEL, and ECHO) can build from.                                     |

| Depth         | 15/20 | There is immense depth on the existing codebase and architecture. The gap is the lack of depth on the *product philosophy* and *user journey* for Maven V2. How will Ruby Red *feel* using this new product? What specific jobs-to-be-done will it solve for her in a new way? |
| Actionability | 13/20 | This is the lowest-scoring dimension. The documents are briefs, not a product roadmap. The AI team cannot directly build from them. To serve Ruby Red, we need a document that provides clear, prioritized guidance on what to build next and why.                                    |

**Total Score: 78/100**

**Threshold Assessment**: The score of 78 is below the 80/100 threshold. The protocol states this requires revision. In this specific case, the "revision" is the primary purpose of this entire Learning Loop execution: to create the Maven Local North Star document that closes these identified gaps. Therefore, we will proceed.

### 2. Prioritized Gap List

This analysis reveals the following gaps that must be addressed to create a certified Maven Local North Star document:

1.  **The "What & Why" Vision**: The documents describe the "how" of the current app but not the "what" and "why" of the future app. We need to define the core product pillars and user-facing value propositions for Maven V2.
2.  **The Emotional Journey Map**: We need to articulate the intended emotional journey for Ruby Red. How do we want her to feel at each stage of using the app, from onboarding to achieving a financial goal?
3.  **The "Surprise & Delight" Factor**: The prompt calls for a "surprise-and-delight product that is like nothing else out there." We need to explicitly define what these innovative, game-changing features will be.
4.  **AI Team Directives**: The document needs to translate the high-level vision into specific directives for each of the six AI team personas. What is BOLT's primary architectural challenge? What is VERA's core psychological principle to uphold?
5.  **A Clear Narrative**: The vision needs a simple, powerful story that ECHO the Storyteller can use to inspire the team and, eventually, the users. The current briefs are analytical; the North Star must be inspirational.
