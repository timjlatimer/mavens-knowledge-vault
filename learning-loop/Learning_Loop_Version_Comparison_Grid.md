**Prepared for**: Tim HB1000 — Chief Visionary Officer, SIC Solve Team

**Purpose**: Side-by-side evaluation of every documented Learning Loop version across eight standardized dimensions, scored 0–100.

## Scoring Methodology

Each version is evaluated across eight dimensions using evidence drawn from the canonical protocol documents, execution summaries, audit reports, and phase ledgers preserved in the `mavens-knowledge-vault`. Scores reflect the state of the protocol **as documented at the time of release**, not retroactive judgments. The weighted overall score uses the following formula:

| Dimension | Weight | Rationale |
|:---|:---:|:---|
| Protocol Design Quality | 15% | How well-written, complete, and anti-drift is the protocol itself |
| Phase Architecture | 15% | How well the phases are structured and sequenced |
| Anti-Drift Mechanisms | 15% | How robust are the mechanisms to prevent AI drift |
| KPI Framework | 10% | How well-defined and measurable are the success metrics |
| Ruby Red Alignment | 15% | How well does this version serve the North Star |
| Execution Effectiveness | 10% | Based on evidence of it being run — what did it actually produce |
| Innovation Delta | 10% | What new capabilities did this version add vs. the previous |
| Overall Score | 10% | Holistic judgment of the version's contribution to the arc |

## Key Findings from the Grid

**Finding 1: The Great Leap occurred between V10.2 and V12.** The weighted score jumped from 70 to 77, representing the transition from a theoretical protocol to an operational platform with a dashboard, credit tracking, and real execution evidence.

**Finding 2: V12.8.1 is the governance watershed.** The introduction of formal TypeScript schemas for drift detection, rollback coordinates, and severity classification transformed the protocol from a set of guidelines into a governance framework.

**Finding 3: V13 (CMS evaluation) is the highest-scoring single execution.** At 95/100 weighted, the Constitutional Memory System evaluation represents the protocol operating at its absolute peak — PRIMAL+ intensity, 22 gaps identified, 16 adversarial conditions resolved, and a complete architectural design for code enforcement.

**Finding 4: V14 scores lower than V13 despite being newer.** This is because V14's ambitious innovations (Swarm Mind, Context Crystallization, 24 enforcement rules) were largely untested at release. The V13 evaluation of V14 found inflated scores and conceptual rather than operational features.

**Finding 5: Ruby Red Alignment shows the most dramatic arc.** From 15/100 in V4 (no mention) to 95/100 in V13 (CMS), the protocol's alignment with its North Star client improved by 80 points across 12 versions.

**Finding 6: Anti-Drift is the dimension with the steepest improvement curve.** From 20/100 in V4 (no mechanisms) to 97/100 in V13 (CMS) (code enforcement architecture), this dimension reflects Tim's persistent concern about AI drift and the team's relentless response.

**Finding 7: Innovation Delta is cyclical, not linear.** Major innovation spikes occur at V4 (foundation), V8.2 (adversarial + evolution), V10 (recursive execution), V13 (persistence), and V14 (crystallization). Between spikes, versions focus on refinement and stabilization.
