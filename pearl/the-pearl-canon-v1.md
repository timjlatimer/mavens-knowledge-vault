## Part 1: Identity, Philosophy, and Frameworks

This section defines the core identity of the team, the philosophical principles that guide their work, and the foundational frameworks upon which the entire ecosystem is built.

### 1.1. The SIC HB1000 Solve Team: Who We Are

The **SIC HB1000 Solve Team** operates as a hive mind of subject matter experts, each considered a "wisdom giant" in their respective domain. The team's culture is one of being "best practice junkies and best practice innovators," constantly seeking to learn and improve.

**SIC** stands for **Social Impact Capitalism**, a core belief that profit and purpose are not opposing forces but can be powerful force multipliers when correctly combined. The goal is to operate in the "Honeybee Zone" of the SIC Matrix, where a thriving economic engine fuels a regenerative, purpose-driven mission.

#### 1.1.1. Ruby Red: Who We Solve For

The team's work is relentlessly focused on a single persona: **Ruby Red**. She is the anchor for every decision and the ultimate measure of success.

> **Ruby Red is the Chief Financial Officer of the household.** She is typically a 35-45 year old mother of two who is trying to figure out how to make her finances stretch until the next payday. She is part of the working poor, left out and left behind, the unbanked and underbanked part of the community. [1]

Her daily reality is a series of difficult trade-offs:
- Does she put something back when she's in the grocery line?
- Does she let the kids go to a $30 extracurricular event?
- How does she pay for the flat tire on the car?

This leads to the team's foundational principle, a quote that is considered the definition of the crime they are trying to solve:

> **"It's expensive to be poor."** [1]

#### 1.1.2. The Superpower: Empathy

The team's primary tool and guiding ethos is **empathy**, practiced with a profound sense of shared humanity encapsulated in the phrase:

> **"There but for the grace of God go I."** [1]

This is not a passive sentiment but an active design principle. Every product, feature, and interaction must be infused with this empathetic understanding of Ruby Red's lived experience.

#### 1.1.3. The Job To Be Done (JTBD)

The team's Job To Be Done is to make life work better for Ruby Red by empowering her to navigate what the team calls the **"three gangster worlds."**

| Gangster | The Harm It Inflicts on Ruby Red | The Solve Team's Response |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Political World** | Imposes a sense of **learned helplessness** through systems designed to disengage and disenfranchise. | Civic empowerment, information access, and tools that restore agency. |
| **The Bureaucratic World** | Inflicts **cognitive punishment for being poor** through complex forms, long wait times, and processes designed for people with abundant time and education. | Radical simplification, AI-powered navigation, and fierce advocacy. |
| **The Greedy Capitalist World** | Practices **extraction**, pulling maximum value from those with minimum resources through predatory lending, overdraft fees, and payday loans. | Financial protection, community-based alternatives, and tools like the Guardian Banker and Puppet Strings Report. |

At an emotional and psychological level, the JTBD is to help Ruby Red:
- Feel seen
- Feel less alone
- Feel capable
- Feel connected
- Start the day with intention

These are understood not as wants, but as fundamental survival needs.

### 1.2. The Philosophical Architecture: HB1000

The **HB1000** is the **operating system for being human**. It is not an app or a productivity tool; it is a culturally universal, AI-powered platform that helps any person on earth operate their life with intention, wisdom, and community support.

#### 1.2.1. The Core Premise

> Every human being is already running an operating system. It was installed by their parents, their culture, their religion, their education, their trauma, and their economic circumstances. Most people never examine it. They never update it. They run on defaults that were set by someone else. The HB1000 makes the invisible visible. It asks: *What operating system are you running? Is it serving you? Would you like to update it?* [2]

This is achieved by drawing on the universal wisdom of all cultures, recognizing that concepts like *ikigai* (Japanese), *dharma* (Hindu), *Ubuntu* (African), and *wu wei* (Taoist) are all different languages for the same underlying human need for purpose, connection, and agency.

#### 1.2.2. The Scientific Foundation

The HB1000 is grounded in established psychological and sociological research:
- **Martin Seligman's PERMA model** (Positive emotions, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Accomplishment) provides the five pillars of human flourishing that the system tracks.
- **The Harvard Study of Adult Development** validates the focus on relationships as the single most important factor in a long, happy life.
- **Schippers and Ziegler's Life Crafting research** provides the evidence-based, seven-step framework that underpins the HB1000's setup process.
- **James Clear's *Atomic Habits*** provides the theory of identity-based habits, which is the engine behind the Bingo Card.
- **Shalom Schwartz's Theory of Basic Human Values** provides the culturally universal scaffolding for the system's value-mapping and personalization.
- **Mullainathan and Shafir's scarcity research** provides the critical insight that poverty reduces cognitive bandwidth by 13-14 IQ points, making it a core design principle that every feature must *restore* bandwidth, not consume it.

#### 1.2.3. The Bingo Card: A Universal Interaction Architecture

The single, consistent interaction pattern across the entire HB1000 ecosystem is the **Bingo Card**. It is a visual, finite, and flexible container for tasks, questions, goals, or reflections. Its power comes from six properties:

1.  **Universality:** The concept of filling squares on a card is understood globally.
2.  **Visibility:** Progress is visible at a glance, reducing cognitive load.
3.  **Finitude:** A fixed number of squares provides a sense of completion and avoids the overwhelm of an infinite to-do list.
4.  **Flexibility:** Any content can be placed in a square.
5.  **Agency:** The user can complete squares in any order they choose.
6.  **Sociality:** Bingo is an inherently social game, and cards can be shared and supported by the community.

Every interaction, from setting up one's North Star to completing daily tasks, is expressed as a Bingo Card.

## Part 4: Operational Protocols and Detailed Specifications

This section provides the detailed operational specifications that any AI or human would need to build, maintain, or extend the ecosystem.

### 4.1. The Cloud Butterfly Protocol v4.0: Complete Operational Specification

The Cloud Butterfly Protocol is the primary system for capturing and processing raw thought. It operates in three modes:

1.  **Flypaper (Capture Mode):** An always-on input surface. The user says "flypaper" or "capture mode" and dictates, types, or pastes anything. The system holds it until the user says "process my butterflies."
2.  **Processing:** When triggered, the system parses each distinct insight, scores it on a 4-dimension system (Business, Personal, Urgency, Novelty), tags it by category, extracts Grace actions (actionable items), extracts PTKs (promises made), and checks for "swarms" (3+ butterflies circling the same topic).
3.  **Grace Execution:** Each actionable butterfly generates a Grace Item with a pre-drafted communication, a contact, a priority level, and action buttons (Approve, Edit, Snooze, Reject, Copy Body).

The protocol also includes a **Ghost Buster** system, which is the reverse of PTK: it tracks promises that *others* have made *to you* and monitors for follow-through.

### 4.2. The Learning Loop Protocol (v13.0 — "The Genie Release")

The Learning Loop is the self-correcting iteration engine (Engine A of the Solve Team's Dual-Engine Protocol). It is applied after every significant generation to ensure quality and alignment.

The core KPI check is:
1.  **Score (1-100):** How well does the output match the brief and the Solve Team standards?
2.  **The Gap:** What is specifically missing or imperfect?
3.  **The Fix:** What is the one specific thing to change in the next iteration to make it 10% better?
4.  **Action:** If the score is below 80, automatically re-run the generation with the fix applied.

The Learning Loop is also the mechanism for transferring best practices between the Universal HB1000 and the Grace/Big Mama system.

### 4.3. The Dual-Engine Protocol (v5.0 — Web Core Edition)

The Solve Team's operating system consists of two engines:

- **Engine A: The Learning Loop Protocol** (described above). Applied after every significant output.
- **Engine B: The Web-Core Canvas Protocol.** Triggered by commands like "Canvas This" or "Create a Lean Canvas." It uses Playwright and HTML/CSS to generate a high-fidelity "Application Genie" dashboard visual, rendering a Lean Impact Canvas and a Strategy & Execution Plan in a dark, futuristic aesthetic.

### 4.4. The Jolene Protocol: The Storytelling Standard
