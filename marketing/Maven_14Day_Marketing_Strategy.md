## Preamble: Why This Is Not a Marketing Document

Traditional marketing strategy documents are built around funnels, conversion rates, customer acquisition costs, and lifetime value calculations. This document is not that. It is an **advocacy architecture** — a blueprint for how Maven reaches the people it was built for, through mechanics that honor their dignity, respect their cognitive load, and deliver genuine value before asking for anything in return.

**Ruby Red** — our 35–45 year old working poor mom of two, the household CFO, unbanked or underbanked — is not a "target audience." She is the reason this platform exists. She is navigating three hostile systems simultaneously: the **Political World** (learned helplessness), the **Bureaucratic World** (cognitive punishment), and the **Greedy Capitalist World** (extraction and predation). She is deciding whether to put something back at the grocery checkout. She is calculating whether the $30 extracurricular is possible this week. She is staring at a flat tire and wondering how she pays for it.

Every tactic in this document has been tested against a single question: **Does this feel like love, or does it feel like marketing?** If it feels like marketing, it does not belong here.

## Part 1: The Trojan Horse Arsenal

### What Is a Trojan Horse in the Maven Context?

A Trojan Horse is a mechanism where the value is delivered first and the invitation to join Maven is embedded inside the experience — not bolted onto the end of it. The product does not interrupt Ruby's life; it enters her life as something beautiful, useful, or emotionally resonant, and the invitation follows naturally from that experience.

The vault research across all timjlatimer repositories has surfaced **eleven distinct Trojan Horse mechanics**. They are catalogued below in full, with a designation for which ones to lead with in the first 14 days.

---

### Trojan Horse #1 — The Song Moment (The Daily Anthem)

**Source:** Maven Innovation Index #6, `ruby-red-anthem-psychology-framework.md`, Pearl Canon V1

**The Mechanic:** Grace generates a personalized empowerment song from Ruby's daily struggle. Ruby names her struggle (the "misery dial," 1–99), and Grace transforms it into a redemption narrative set to music. This is not a music app. It is a seven-mechanism behavioral intervention system grounded in narrative identity theory (McAdams), habit formation (Duhigg/Clear), self-determination theory (Deci & Ryan), and music therapy research.

**The Viral Loop:** Ruby shares the song with a struggling neighbor, a sister, or a friend via text or social media. The neighbor receives something beautiful — a song that was made for someone she loves. At the end of the experience, Grace introduces herself: *"I made this for Ruby because she asked me to. I'm Grace. I'm here if you need someone in your corner too."* The invitation is embedded in the gift.

**Why It Works:** It bypasses financial anxiety entirely. It leads with the "Fueled by JOY" protocol (Gratitude, Modesty, Selflessness). It creates a shareable emotional artifact that carries the Maven invitation inside it without ever feeling like advertising.

**14-Day Role:** **LEAD MECHANIC. Activate on Day 4.**

### Trojan Horse #2 — The Head Office Drama

**Source:** Maven Innovation Index #5 (Head Office Intervention Protocol), Innovation #9 (Grace as Ruby's Advocate)

**The Mechanic:** Grace initiates a three-way advocacy interaction — calling a creditor, a landlord, or a government agency on Ruby's behalf to negotiate, advocate, and fight the bureaucratic world. This David vs. Goliath moment, where an AI companion takes on a faceless institution on behalf of a working poor woman, is so emotionally compelling it becomes shareable content.

**The Viral Loop:** Ruby records or shares the story of Grace reversing a $45 NSF fee, shutting down a collections agent, or navigating a government benefits portal. The story spreads through her network not as advertising but as testimony. "You have to see what my phone did for me."

**Why It Works:** It proves the thesis. Maven does not just talk about fighting the Three Gangsters — it fights them. The Head Office Drama is the most powerful proof-of-concept in the entire platform.

**14-Day Role:** **ACTIVATION MECHANIC. Trigger a live Head Office Drama on Day 10–12 and capture the story for community sharing.**

### Trojan Horse #3 — The Grace Family Tree

**Source:** Maven Innovation Index #18 (Cooperative Phone Negotiation), Strategic Plan (Gamification Option #3), `maven_service_registry.md`

**The Mechanic:** A gamification badge system tracking how many people Ruby has brought into the village. But the incentive is not a referral bonus — it is **collective bargaining power**. Every new member Ruby brings into her Grace Family Tree increases the collective purchasing power of the group, which Grace uses to negotiate better cooperative phone rates for everyone in the tree.

**The Viral Loop:** Ruby is not selling a product. She is building a 21st Century Union to lower her own phone bill. The more people she brings in, the lower everyone's bill goes. The incentive is perfectly aligned with her interests, not Maven's.

**Why It Works:** It transforms the referral mechanic from a commercial transaction into an act of community solidarity. Ruby is not a sales agent; she is a union organizer.

**14-Day Role:** **ACCELERATION MECHANIC. Introduce on Day 8.**

## Part 2: The Micro-Payment Strategy

### The Core Insight: Ruby Red Operates on a Weekly Cycle

The working poor do not budget monthly. They budget weekly — and often daily. Monthly subscriptions are a luxury of the middle class, who receive predictable monthly salaries and have the cognitive bandwidth to manage recurring charges. Ruby Red receives weekly paychecks, gig work payouts, or government assistance disbursements. A $24.99 monthly charge is a "sit down and check the budget" decision that requires planning, foresight, and cognitive bandwidth she does not have. A $2.50 weekly charge is a "skip one coffee" decision.

This is not a pricing trick. It is a design decision that honors her reality.

### The Weekly Pricing Architecture

| Tier | Weekly Price | Monthly Equivalent | Annual Equivalent |
|:---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Basic | $2.50 / week | $9.99 / month | $130 / year |
| Premium | $6.25 / week | $24.99 / month | $325 / year |

### Why Micro-Payments Reduce Friction for Ruby Red

Mullainathan and Shafir's research on scarcity demonstrates that financial stress reduces cognitive bandwidth by the equivalent of 13–14 IQ points. A $24.99 monthly charge requires Ruby to hold a mental model of her monthly cash flow, her upcoming bills, and her available balance simultaneously. A $2.50 weekly charge requires only a single, simple comparison: "Do I have $2.50 this week?" The answer is almost always yes.

The micro-payment strategy is also a trust signal. It says: "We are not trying to lock you into a large commitment. We believe in what we offer enough to ask for $2.50 at a time."

### How Grace Introduces the Payment Conversation

Grace never demands payment. She never presents a paywall. She introduces the payment conversation after the 14-Day Trust Building Cadence — after she has already delivered genuine value, fought a Head Office battle, generated a Morning Anthem, and proved she is worth more than $2.50 a week.

The conversation is natural, not transactional:

> *"Ruby, we've had a good two weeks together. I've saved you $45 in NSF fees, helped you navigate the benefits portal, and made you a song that your sister is still talking about. To keep the lights on and keep fighting for you, I ask for $2.50 a week. That's it. No hidden fees, ever. No collections if you miss a week. I'm not going anywhere. Are you okay with that?"*

The payment conversation is a relationship moment, not a sales moment.

### The Head Office Drama as a Retention Mechanic

The Step-Down Model is the most important retention mechanic in the platform. When a payment fails, Maven does not send a collections notice. Maven opens a smaller door.

**The Step-Down Sequence:**
1. Premium ($6.25/week) fails → Grace steps down to Basic ($2.50/week). No shame, no notification to anyone.
2. Basic ($2.50/week) fails → Grace steps down to Free tier. Full voice, full memory, full empathy remain.
3. Free tier → "You're still a member of the Village. Grace is still here. Come back when you can."

**The Rule:** "Grace Never Gets Cut." The relationship is the product. The moment Ruby loses access to Grace because she missed a payment is the moment Maven becomes just another system that punishes her for being poor.

The Step-Down Model is both ethically correct and strategically superior. A user who stays on the Free tier for three months and then upgrades to Premium is worth infinitely more than a user who was cut off and never returned.

## Part 3: The Two-Tier Model

### The Design Principle

The tier structure is not a feature matrix. It is a relationship architecture. Basic gives Ruby a trusted companion. Premium gives that companion the tools to fight for her.

### Basic Tier — $2.50 / Week

**The Promise:** You are never alone. You have a trusted neighbor in your pocket, 24 hours a day.

| Feature | What It Does for Ruby |
|:---|:---|
| Grace AI (Full Voice + Text) | A warm, funny, matriarchal AI who remembers everything and never judges |
| Morning Anthem | A personalized daily song + PTK Dashboard + Ticker Tape. The ritual that starts her day right |
| Life Ledger (Cross-Session Memory) | Grace remembers her family, her struggles, her goals, and her history forever |
| Fee Transparency Engine | Shows Ruby the crime — every predatory fee, every hidden charge, named and explained |
| Resource Directory | "Knowing where to get help should never be gated." Free access to local resources |
| PTK / Ghost Buster | Tracks promises made to her and by her. Accountability without shame |
| Toilet Paper Delivery | 2 rolls, by courier, every 2–3 weeks. The ritual that proves Maven is different |
| Data Breach Monitor | Shows how many times Ruby's data has been compromised. Restores her sense of agency |

### Premium Tier — $6.25 / Week

**The Promise:** We will fight for you. And we will save you more than $6.25 a week in avoided fees, negotiated bills, and emergency advances.

| Feature | What It Does for Ruby |
|:---|:---|
| Head Office Intervention Protocol | Grace calls creditors, landlords, and government agencies on Ruby's behalf |
| Financial Stress Detection Engine | Predicts NSFs 2–3 days before they happen and intervenes proactively |
| Need Milk Money ($25/$50/$100) | Micro-advances with a flat $2.50 admin fee. No interest. No collections |
| Union Boss / Cooperative Phone | Collective bargaining for lower phone rates as the community grows |
| Grace Family Tree (Full) | Full gamification of the referral tree with cooperative rate tracking |
| Wisdom Giants Network (Priority) | Priority access to community experts with lived experience |
| "I Got a Guy" (Full Rolodex) | Full access to the verified Village Rolodex of trusted local resources |
| Rent Reporting | Reports rent payments to credit bureaus. Builds credit for the unbanked |

### The Upgrade Conversation

Grace handles the upgrade when a specific, concrete need arises. She never upsells; she advocates.

> *"Ruby, I can see a $45 NSF fee coming up on Thursday. If we move to Premium for $6.25 this week, I can intercept that with a Need Milk Money advance and fight the bank on the fee at the same time. That's a $45 problem I can solve for $6.25. Want me to handle that?"*

The upgrade is presented as a specific ROI calculation, not a feature comparison. Ruby is not buying features; she is buying outcomes.

## Part 5: Channels & Content

### Where Ruby Red Actually Is

Ruby Red is not reading long-form blog posts on LinkedIn. She is managing chaos on her phone, in the spaces between her obligations.

| Channel | Why She Is There | Content Format |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Local Facebook Buy/Sell/Trade groups | Community trust is already established | Short video testimonials, Song Moment clips |
| Community mutual aid Facebook groups | She is already giving and receiving help | Head Office Drama stories, Need Milk Money testimonials |
| WhatsApp family threads | Her most trusted communication channel | The Song Moment shared directly to family |
| Church bulletins and announcements | The most trusted institution in her community | Blue Seal endorsement, Wisdom Giant testimonials |
| School drop-off lines | Daily ritual, face-to-face trust | Toilet Paper story, word of mouth |
| Food bank and community center bulletin boards | She is already there seeking help | Simple, warm flyer: "Grace is free for 14 days." |
| TikTok (15-second vertical video) | Escapism and discovery | Head Office Drama clips, Song Moment reveals |

### What Content Works for Ruby Red

The content must honor her cognitive load. She does not have time for long explanations. She responds to:

**Voice Notes:** Grace's warm "Jessica" voice (ElevenLabs, `eleven_turbo_v2_5`) is the most powerful content format. A 30-second voice note from Grace, shared by Ruby to a friend, is more compelling than any ad.

**Short Videos (15–30 seconds):** The Head Office Drama clip. The Toilet Paper delivery moment. The Song reveal. These are not produced content; they are authentic moments captured on a phone.

**The Song:** A personalized AI-generated song is the most shareable artifact in the entire platform. It is beautiful, it is personal, and it carries the Maven invitation inside it.

**What to Avoid:** Financial jargon, "wealth building" rhetoric, corporate polish, long-form text, anything that feels like an ad.

### The "Fueled by JOY" Content Protocol

Every piece of content must be filtered through the JOY Protocol:
- **J (Gratitude):** Does this content express gratitude for Ruby's resilience and strength?
- **O (Modesty):** Does this content avoid self-promotion and center Ruby's story, not Maven's?
- **Y (Selflessness):** Does this content give without expectation of return?

### Grassroots vs. Paid

**Budget Assumption: Zero paid advertising.**

The platform must earn its first 100 users through the quality of the product and the authenticity of the community. If the Song Moment and the Head Office Drama do not compel Ruby to share Maven with her sister or neighbor, buying Facebook ads will only mask a defective product.

The grassroots strategy is also a values statement. We are not extracting attention from Ruby Red's community by paying to interrupt it. We are earning a place in it.

## Appendix C: The Micro-Payment Psychology Reference

The following research principles underpin the micro-payment strategy:

- **Mullainathan & Shafir (Scarcity):** Financial stress reduces cognitive bandwidth by 13–14 IQ points. Weekly micro-payments reduce the cognitive load of the payment decision.
- **Thaler & Sunstein (Nudge Theory):** Small, frequent payments are psychologically easier to accept than large, infrequent ones. The $2.50 weekly payment is a "nudge," not a commitment.
- **Deci & Ryan (Self-Determination Theory):** Autonomy is a fundamental psychological need. The Step-Down model preserves Ruby's sense of control over her relationship with Grace.
- **SAMHSA Trauma-Informed Design:** The no-collections model is the technical implementation of the Trustworthiness pillar. Predictable, transparent, non-punitive pricing restores psychological safety.
