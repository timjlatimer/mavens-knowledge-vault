# The Jolene Protocol — 500-Word Version

**Origin:** Derived from Emmy-winning journalist Jolene Latimer's feature work. This is the compressed reference for Grade-A narrative journalism.

---

## The Non-Negotiables

**Opening Hook:** First sentence does the work of a paragraph. Image first, tension immediate, no throat-clearing.
- *Example:* "Two garbage bags. That's what Nicole carried when she finally left."

**Physical Object:** One concrete thing that carries the weight of the theme. Introduce early, call back at close.
- *Examples:* Sooty taillights, garbage bags, toilet paper up two flights of stairs

**Quote Discipline:** One great quote per source. No piling. Use the line the subject hands you.

**The Kicker:** End on the line that stays. Simple. Final. Often callbacks to the opening. Know when to stop.
- *Example:* "Two garbage bags. A careful message. A village that answered."

**Restraint:** Trust the material more than the reader. Never sentimentalize. Let the story do the emotional work.

---

## Structure (1,200 words)

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Act 1 | Establish what's at stake or what was lost |
| Act 2 | Sit in the difficulty (don't rush) |
| Turn (~60%) | The hinge moment where everything shifts |
| Act 3 | Resolution — grace arrives, insight earned |
| Close | Callback to opening, then stop |

---

## Structure (500 words)

| Section | Words | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Hook + scene entry | 75-100 | Drop into moment with object |
| Context/tension | 150-175 | Who, stakes, why it matters |
| Turn | 125-150 | The thing that changes everything |
| Resolution + kicker | 50-75 | The line that stays |

**At 500 words:** One story, one object, one turn, one kicker. That's it.

---

## Voice Principles

1. **Warm but not soft** — affection without protection
2. **Trust the material** — don't explain what the scene shows
3. **Theme earned, not stated** — "dignity" is shown, never named
4. **Thematic ambition** — not a profile of a person, but an essay about an idea that happens to have a person in it

---

## What Jolene Never Does

- Overexplain transitions
- Sentimentalize
- Let the middle run long
- State the theme before it's earned
- Pile quotes
- End with marketing language or an ask

---

## Grading Checklist

| Element | A Grade Standard |
|---------|------------------|
| Hook | Image first, immediate tension |
| Object | Present, early, called back |
| Quotes | One per source, no piling |
| Restraint | No sentimentality |
| Scene | Stays in moment, not summary |
| Theme | Earned through action |
| Kicker | Callback, knows when to stop |

**A- Flaws (fixable):**
- Naming the theme explicitly ("dignity arrived")
- Manifesto language intruding on narrative
- Generalizations ("the way most people do")

---

## Final Tests

1. Read last three sentences. Can you cut two? Do it.
2. Read first paragraph. Can you start later? Do it.
3. Remove every adjective. Add back only the essential ones.
4. Does every sentence belong to the narrative, not to a manifesto?

---

## The Core Truth

Jolene doesn't write profiles of people.

She writes essays about ideas that happen to have people in them.

The subject is the vehicle. The theme is the destination.
