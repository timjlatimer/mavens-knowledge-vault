## Executive Summary

Andrej Karpathy is one of the most consequential figures in the democratization of artificial intelligence. A founding member of OpenAI, former Director of AI at Tesla, and now founder of Eureka Labs, Karpathy has spent the last two years systematically open-sourcing the tools that make frontier AI research accessible to individuals with a single GPU and an internet connection. His latest project, **autoresearch**, released in March 2026, allows AI agents to autonomously run machine learning experiments overnight — modifying code, training models, evaluating results, and iterating without human intervention. In two days, the system ran 650 experiments and discovered improvements that transferred from small models to large ones, putting his nanochat project on track for a new GPT-2 benchmark result [1].

This brief argues that Karpathy's body of work represents a **Move 37** for the SIC HB1000 Solve Team — an unconventional, high-leverage strategic opportunity that aligns directly with our mission to empower Ruby Red and dismantle the structural barriers that make it expensive to be poor.

## Who Is Andrej Karpathy?

Andrej Karpathy (born October 23, 1986) is a Slovakian-Canadian AI researcher whose career reads like a roadmap of the modern AI revolution. He completed his PhD at Stanford under Fei-Fei Li, where he designed and taught CS 231n — the first deep learning class at Stanford, which grew from 150 to 750 enrolled students and became one of the most influential courses in the field [2]. His career trajectory tells the story of someone who has been at the center of every major AI inflection point.

| Period | Role | Significance |
|--------|------|-------------|
| 2011-2015 | PhD, Stanford (under Fei-Fei Li) | Pioneered computer vision + NLP intersection |
| 2015-2017 | Research Scientist, OpenAI (founding team) | Helped build the organization that created GPT |
| 2017-2022 | Director of AI, Tesla | Led Autopilot, Full Self-Driving, Tesla Optimus |
| 2023-2024 | Research Scientist, OpenAI (return) | Midtraining and synthetic data generation |
| 2024-present | Founder, Eureka Labs | "AI Native School" — democratizing AI education |

What makes Karpathy uniquely relevant to the SIC mission is not just his technical credentials — it is his **philosophical orientation**. He is a builder who believes in making powerful technology accessible to ordinary people, and he has consistently chosen to open-source his most important work rather than lock it behind corporate walls.

## "Power to the People" — The Ruby Red Alignment

In April 2025, Karpathy published a blog post titled "Power to the People: How LLMs Flip the Script on Technology Diffusion" that reads like it was written for the SIC Solve Team [4]. His central thesis is that LLMs represent a **dramatic reversal** of the normal pattern of technology diffusion. Historically, transformative technologies flow top-down: from government and military contexts, through corporations, and eventually reaching individuals. Electricity, cryptography, computers, flight, the internet, GPS — all followed this path.

LLMs broke the pattern. As Karpathy writes:

> "So it strikes me as quite unique and remarkable that LLMs display a dramatic reversal of this pattern — they generate disproportionate benefit for regular people, while their impact is a lot more muted and lagging in corporations and governments."

He explains why this matters for ordinary people — for Ruby Red:

> "An individual will usually only be an expert in at most one thing, so the broad quasi-expertise offered by the LLM fundamentally allows them to do things they couldn't do before. People can now vibe code apps. They can approach legal documents. They can grok esoteric research papers. They can do data analytics."

This is precisely the empowerment that the SIC HB1000 is designed to deliver. When Ruby Red — the 35-45 year old mom trying to make her finances stretch until the next payday — can use AI to understand a legal document, analyze her budget, navigate bureaucratic forms, or build a simple tool for her household, the cost of being poor drops. The "three gangster worlds" (political, bureaucratic, greedy capitalist) lose some of their power over her.

But Karpathy also sounds a warning that maps directly to our urgency:

> "The moment money can buy dramatically better ChatGPT, things change. Large organizations get to concentrate their vast resources to buy more intelligence. And within the category of 'individual' too, the elite may once again split away from the rest of society. Their child will be tutored by GPT-8-pro-max-high, yours by GPT-6 mini."

This is the window. Right now, the playing field is more level than it has ever been. Karpathy's open-source work is a force that keeps it level. The SIC's job is to build the bridges that help Ruby Red cross before the window closes.

## The Move 37 Strategy: How to Incorporate Karpathy's Work

In Go, Move 37 was the move that no human expected — the move that revealed a fundamentally different way of seeing the board. Karpathy's open-model ecosystem represents a Move 37 for the SIC because it offers a path to AI capability that bypasses the traditional gatekeepers entirely.

### Strategic Integration Points

**1. Maven's AI Backbone — Leverage nanochat/nanoGPT**

Maven, the SIC's technology platform, could incorporate Karpathy's open-source training pipelines to build custom, fine-tuned AI models specifically optimized for Ruby Red's needs. Rather than depending entirely on commercial API providers (who can change pricing, terms, or capabilities at any time), the team could train specialized models for financial literacy, benefits navigation, bureaucratic form completion, and household budgeting — all running on modest hardware.

**2. The Learning Loop — Adopt the autoresearch Paradigm**

The autoresearch concept maps directly onto the SIC's Learning Loop protocol. Karpathy's system works by writing research strategy in a Markdown file (`program.md`) while the AI agent executes, iterates, and improves autonomously. This is functionally identical to how the SIC's Learning Loop operates: the human team sets the strategic direction, and the AI system iterates toward improvement. The SIC should study autoresearch's architecture as a reference implementation for autonomous improvement cycles.

**3. Eureka Labs Model — Education as Empowerment**

Karpathy's Eureka Labs vision — "Teacher + AI symbiosis" where human experts design course materials and AI teaching assistants scale the delivery — is a blueprint for how the SIC could deliver financial literacy, benefits education, and civic empowerment to Ruby Red. The LLM101n course structure (open-source materials + AI tutoring) could be adapted for "Financial Literacy 101n" or "Benefits Navigation 101n."

**4. The "program.md" Paradigm — Democratizing Strategy**

Perhaps the most profound insight from autoresearch is that the human's job shifts from writing code to writing strategy in plain language. The `program.md` file is essentially a "skill" — a set of instructions that guides the AI agent's behavior. This is exactly how the SIC's skill system works. Karpathy has independently validated the architectural pattern that the SIC is already building.

## Why This Is a Move 37

The conventional approach to AI for social impact is to license commercial APIs, build applications on top of them, and hope the pricing stays affordable. That approach leaves Ruby Red dependent on the same corporate gatekeepers who have historically made it expensive to be poor.

Karpathy's open-model ecosystem offers a fundamentally different path. It is a path where the SIC can build, train, and deploy AI models that are optimized specifically for the people we serve — models that understand the language of benefits applications, the complexity of household budgeting on irregular income, the maze of bureaucratic forms. Models that run on modest hardware, cost almost nothing to operate, and cannot be taken away by a corporate pricing change.

The move nobody expects is this: **a social impact organization building its own AI capability using open-source tools, guided by the same philosophy that the most respected AI researcher in the world is championing — power to the people.**

Karpathy wrote: "The future is already here, and it is shockingly distributed." [4]

The SIC's job is to make sure Ruby Red is part of that distribution.
