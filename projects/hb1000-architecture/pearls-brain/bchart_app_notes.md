# B-Chart App — Extracted Knowledge
**Source:** https://bchartapp-weurubcf.manus.space
**Date:** 2026-02-14

## What It Is
The B-Chart Interactive is an "Attitude × Skill Performance Matrix" — a people assessment tool.
- Tagline: "Build the People, They'll Build the Engine" — From Seed to North Star
- Powered by: The Latimer Douglas Protocol · Social Impact Capitalism

## Four Quadrants
1. **Honey Bee** (High Attitude, High Skill) — "Advocates of Values & Purpose" → Recommended: Promote & Develop
2. **Worker Bee** (High Attitude, Lower Skill) — "Friends of Values"
3. **Drone** (Low Attitude, Lower Skill) — "Skeptics of Values & Purpose"
4. **SOB** (Low Attitude, High Skill) — "Blockers of Values"

## Key Zones
- **Sweet Spot** — top performers in both dimensions
- **Flight Risk** — "Future Entrepreneurs" — high performers likely to leave without growth opportunities
- **Up & Coming** — developing talent

## Assessment Dimensions (6 total)
1. Attitude (out of 40)
2. Leadership (out of 35)
3. Time Management (out of 35)
4. Performance (out of 40)
5. Communication (out of 38)
6. Values & Purpose (out of 35)

## Multi-Perspective Assessment
- Manager view
- Employee (self) view
- Peer view
- Gap analysis between perspectives

## Features
- TTS Assessment (30-question Temperament Test — feeds into B-Chart)
- Team Overview (all employees on one chart — spot patterns and risks)
- Flight Path tracking (historical movement on the chart)
- Two display modes: Hive (dark) and Illustrated (light)

## TTS Assessment Structure (30 Questions)
The assessment has 6 categories, 5 questions each:
1. **Values & Purpose** (5 questions)
2. **Leadership & Initiative** (5 questions)
3. **Attitude & Engagement** (5 questions)
4. **Technical Performance** (5 questions)
5. **Time Management** (5 questions)
6. **Communication** (5 questions)

Three assessment perspectives: Manager, Self, Peer
Assessment periods: Quarterly (Q1 2026, Q4 2025, etc.)
