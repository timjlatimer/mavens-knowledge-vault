1. **Poverty is a cognitive condition, not just a financial one.** Mullainathan & Shafir proved scarcity reduces cognitive bandwidth by 13-14 IQ points. Every HB1000 feature must RESTORE bandwidth, not consume it.

2. **Seven psychological mechanisms** operate simultaneously in the system:
   - Narrative Identity Construction (McAdams)
   - Habit Formation (Duhigg, Clear)
   - Self-Determination (Deci & Ryan)
   - Social Proof & Belonging (Cialdini)
   - Emotional Regulation (Music Therapy)
   - Community Protection (Puppet Strings)
   - Behavioral Nudging (Thaler & Sunstein)

3. **The Daily Anthem is a behavioral intervention system disguised as a creative tool.** This design principle applies to the entire HB1000 — it must feel like something enjoyable, not something clinical.

4. **The Puppet Strings Report** measures manipulation exposure: Fear, Scarcity, Social Proof, Authority Bias, Harvest Level, Mental Manipulation Exposure, Joy Hijack Alert. This is the protection layer.

5. **The three gangster worlds** are formally defined: Political (learned helplessness), Bureaucratic (cognitive punishment for being poor), Greedy Capitalist (extracting maximum value from minimum resources).

6. **The JTBD for Ruby Red**: Feel seen, feel less alone, feel capable, feel connected, start the day with intention. These are survival needs, not entertainment needs.

7. **30 academic references** ground the entire framework in peer-reviewed research.
