# Destiny Discovered Framework Analysis

## Overview
Destiny Discovered is a personal development program focused on helping individuals discover their "sense of destiny" - what makes great people great. The program is based on 30 years of research studying over 1,600 biographies of great leaders and historical figures.

## Core Philosophy
- Life needs three things: sense of purpose, opportunity to contribute, developing legacy
- What makes the great, great is a **sense of destiny**
- Great people have personal rules/code that govern their existence
- Philosophy of life creates balance and guidance for decisions

## Program Structure
**Lesson 1: Destiny Support System**
- Focus on creating a personal philosophy of life
- Understanding personal rules and belief systems
- Examining what drives and controls life decisions

## Key Questions/Data Points to Collect

### Philosophy & Values (Questions 1-8)
1. Most important ingredient in life (beyond loved ones and health)
2. Characteristic admired most in others that you'd like to have
3. Three things to change in behavior
4. Influences out of control
5. Influences under control
6. Three most driving and controlling influences determining continuation of universe
7. Three limiting factors in life
8. Rate in order of importance: body, mind, spirit

### Purpose & Contribution (Questions 9-12)
9. Opportunity to develop meaningful contribution (Bad/Good/Excellent)
10. Three most important things for success advice
11. Three things life should stand for
12. Three things not prepared to do

### Self-Perception & Future (Questions 13-14)
13. How others rate future possibility (Outstanding/Average/Poor)
14. Importance of money/material wealth (Yes/No/Take it or leave it)

### Action & Impact (Questions 15-18)
15. Six measurable ways to change world without money
16. Six measurable ways to change world with money
17. Obligation to do points from 15 and 16
18. Success possibilities of schemes/dreams (Good/Fair/Bad)

### Beliefs & Absolutes (Questions 19-21)
19. Limitations/preferences regarding culture, race, religion, behavior patterns (Yes/No with reasons)
20. Six absolutes that govern thoughts and actions
21. How to justify absolutes

## Data Collection Purpose
Creating a "North Star database" of insights from people's responses to understand:
- Personal philosophies and belief systems
- Values and priorities
- Sense of purpose and destiny
- Barriers and opportunities
- Action orientation and commitment levels

## Complete Question List (30 Questions)

### Questions 22-30 (Additional Deep Questions)
22. If you believe in God or Superior Being (Why, Advantages, Disadvantages, Evidence of existence)
23. If you don't believe in God or Superior Being (Why, Advantages, Disadvantages, Evidence of existence)
24. How do you rate the times you live in for opportunity for success? (Excellent/Good/Fair/Bad) + 3 reasons + Are others proving you right/wrong?
25. What makes successful people successful? (Multiple choice: inherited wealth, gifted mind, unusual opportunities, special assistance, personal development, hard work, lucky break, strong principles, good plan, good idea, other factors)
26. Three people to assist in life's adventure and reasons
27. Responsibility to meet obligations if let down in personal relationship (None/Some/Total/Other + Why)
28. Obligation if other party doesn't fulfill agreement in business (None/Some/Total/Other + Why)
29. Obligation to assist truly disadvantaged (Yes/No + Why/How)
30. Percentage breakdown of success factors (Personal influence, Influence of others, Economic/social influences, Uncontrollable influences = 100%)

## Sample Response Data

**Respondent:** Jessie Moores, Director of Client Care Operations

**Key Insights from Sample Response:**

**Core Values & Philosophy:**
- Most important ingredient: Faith (shapes values, guides decisions, gives purpose)
- Admired characteristic: Confidence without arrogance, humility, comfort with not knowing
- Priority order: Spirit - Mind - Body

**Behavioral Changes Desired:**
1. Trust self more at work, stop second-guessing
2. More understanding and patient with family
3. Read more, grow in faith, prioritize what's truly important

**Control & Influences:**
- Out of control: Others' choices, unexpected events, societal pressures
- Under control: Work competence, life perspective, gratitude focus
- Driving influences: Faith, Selflessness, Empathy ("there for the grace of God go I")
- Limiting factors: Second-guessing at work, desire for approval, impulse management

**Purpose & Legacy:**
- Life should stand for: Faith and gratitude, Love and compassion for family, Integrity/empathy/grace
- Not prepared to: Purposely hurt someone, Do something illegal/unethical, Compromise faith
- Future possibility rating: Outstanding (with humility)

**Action Orientation:**
- Without money: Raise children with values, Mentor others, Acts of kindness, Share knowledge, Lead with integrity, Build relationships
- With money: Feed people in need, Fund education/missionaries, Support housing/shelters, Provide life experiences for underserved, Help orphanages, Fund healthcare
- Feels obligation to all actions in question 15 and first 2 in question 16

**Six Absolutes:**
1. Faith in God
2. Acting with integrity and staying true to values
3. Showing love and compassion
4. Practicing gratitude
5. Committing to continuous growth
6. Treating people with empathy and understanding

**Justifications:**
- Gives purpose and guidance, keeps grounded
- Ensures honesty and accountability, builds trust
- Shapes interactions, fosters healthy relationships
- Keeps focused on what you have vs. lack
- Pushes to learn and improve, understand others

**Belief in God:**
- Why: "How can there be a creation without a Creator. I can't not believe."
- Advantages: Peace, security, love, compassion, hope, being part of something, forgiveness
- Evidence: The Bible (never been disproven), documented accounts of Jesus

**Success Factors:**
- Good opportunity for meaningful contribution
- Success possibilities of dreams: Good
- Three reasons for success: Great team who believe in goals, Common purpose and values, Clarity and focus on priorities

**Success Attributes:**
- Hard work
- Dedication and determination
- Passion and compassion
- Agility and adaptability
- Opportunity

**Success Factor Breakdown (Question 30):**
- Personal influence: 40%
- Influence of others: 20%
- Economic/social: 30%
- Uncontrollable: 10%
