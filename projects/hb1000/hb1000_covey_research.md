# Covey Research — HB1000 Foundation Layer

## Key Concepts from Habit 2: Begin with the End in Mind

### The Funeral Exercise
- Imagine your own funeral. Who's there? What do they say about you?
- This distills what you ultimately VALUE in life
- Eulogy virtues vs. résumé virtues (David Brooks distinction)
- Résumé virtues: skills, job titles, money, external success
- Eulogy virtues: character, relationships, kindness, integrity — the deeper ones
- Most people WANT eulogy virtues to drive their actions but get diverted by "scripts"

### Scripts
- Default goals and values handed to us by social systems (family, peers, school, ads, culture)
- Scripts cause us to "should on ourselves" and participate in status competitions we don't care about
- Result: accomplish a lot but feel life devoid of significance
- Example: Get good grades → good college → good job → house/car/vacations — but no deeper purpose
- Tolstoy at 51: worldly success but hollow — hadn't lived by his own values

### The Personal Mission Statement
- Covey's answer to scripts: write your OWN script — a personal mission statement
- Not something you write overnight — requires deep introspection
- Two elements: (1) what you want to DO/accomplish, (2) who you want to BE (character)
- Should be based on PRINCIPLES, not on people, possessions, or circumstances
- Becomes your personal constitution — the DNA of all other decisions

### Roles as the Operating System
- Covey says: identify your key ROLES in life
- Typical roles: Self, Spouse/Partner, Parent, Professional, Community Member, Friend
- Each role gets its own mission — what does success look like in this role?
- Weekly planning organized BY ROLE — not just a to-do list
- "The trail you leave behind" — what will people say about you in each role?

### The Connection to HB1000
- The HB1000 IS the personal mission statement made operational
- It's not just a document — it's a LIVING SYSTEM that Pearl reads and acts on
- Covey's roles = Foundation tab
- Covey's mission statement = North Star

- The funeral exercise = "The Trail You Leave Behind" in each role
