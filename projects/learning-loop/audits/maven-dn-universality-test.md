**Smelling Salts Verdict**: ✅ PASS

---

### Move 37 Extreme

**Conventional approach**: Simplify forms, reduce steps, add progress bars, use plain language.

**Village-powered approaches**:

1. **The Trust Ambassador**: What if Maria's friend who already uses Maven DN could send her a personalized invite that pre-fills her info and walks her through setup together via split-screen? Trust transfers through relationships, not UI design.

2. **The Cash Bridge**: Maria pays for everything in cash. What if onboarding started at the corner store where she already buys money orders? The cashier helps her download and set up Maven DN right there, and her first deposit is the cash she was going to use for a money order anyway.

3. **The Story Onboarding**: Instead of forms, Maria sees a 60-second video of someone like her — same age, same situation — explaining how they use Maven DN. Then the app asks: "Want to do what she did?" One button. The forms come later, after trust is built.

4. **The Buddy System**: Pair every new unbanked user with a "Maven Guide" — an existing user in their neighborhood who gets a small reward for helping them through the first 30 days. Not customer support. A neighbor.

5. **The Zero-Risk Start**: Let Maria use Maven DN for 30 days without linking any bank account or providing her SSN. She can receive money, track spending, and see how it works. When she's ready to trust it, THEN she provides sensitive info.
