# Kokanee Springs RV Village — Entrance Sign Construction Specifications

**Project:** Renovation of Existing Entrance Gateway Sign  
**Location:** 53118 Highway 31, Seba Beach, Parkland County, Alberta  
**Date:** February 12, 2026  
**Version:** V4.3 Final  

---

## 1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

This specification covers the renovation and cladding of the existing timber entrance gateway at Kokanee Springs RV Village. The project reuses the existing post structure as the substructure, cladding it with new materials and adding a large arched sign panel across the top. No new footings or permit-required structures are involved — this is a renovation of the existing sign in its current location.

## 3. PILLAR CLUSTER SPECIFICATIONS

Each side of the gateway has a **pillar cluster** consisting of a main pillar and a secondary pillar mounted side by side.

### 3.1 Main Pillar (x2 — one per side)

| Specification | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Finished Outer Dimensions** | 24 in x 24 in (61 cm x 61 cm) square |
| **Height** | 12 ft (3.66 m) from grade to cap |
| **Construction Method** | Clad over existing timber posts using 2x6 or 2x8 framing to create a box, then sheathed with 3/4-inch exterior-grade plywood or 1x8 tongue-and-groove cedar boards |
| **Inner Substructure** | Existing round timber posts (approx. 10-12 in diameter) — retained as structural core |
| **Cladding Material** | Option A: 1x8 tongue-and-groove Western Red Cedar boards, vertical orientation. Option B: 3/4-inch exterior-grade MDO (Medium Density Overlay) plywood for smooth painted finish |
| **Base** | 30 in x 30 in (76 cm x 76 cm) flared base skirt, 18 in tall, built from matching cladding material over 2x6 framing. Creates a visual foundation and hides the existing post base/concrete |
| **Cap** | 28 in x 28 in (71 cm x 71 cm) pyramid cap, built from 2x material and capped with copper or painted aluminum flashing. Solar light mounted on top center |

### 3.2 Secondary Pillar (x2 — one per side)

| Specification | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Finished Outer Dimensions** | 18 in x 18 in (46 cm x 46 cm) square |
| **Height** | 10 ft (3.05 m) from grade to cap — shorter than main pillar |
| **Position** | Mounted directly adjacent to the main pillar on the outer side, with a 2-inch gap between them |
| **Construction Method** | Freestanding built-up box column using 4x4 pressure-treated post as core, clad with matching material |
| **Footing** | 12 in diameter x 48 in deep sonotube with post anchor bracket (frost depth for Parkland County) |

### 3.3 Pillar Cluster Assembly

The main pillar and secondary pillar on each side are connected with a horizontal timber tie at the 8 ft level (hidden behind the cladding) and a diagonal brace from the secondary pillar to the main pillar at the sign panel connection point. This provides lateral stability for the overhead sign panel.

## 4. SIGN PANEL SPECIFICATIONS

### 4.1 Panel Structure

| Specification | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Shape** | Arched — flat bottom edge, curved top following a gentle arch |
| **Width** | 18 ft (5.49 m) — spans between outer edges of main pillar caps |
| **Height at Center (peak)** | 8 ft (2.44 m) from bottom edge to arch peak |
| **Height at Edges (where it meets pillars)** | 4 ft (1.22 m) — the arch springs from this height |
| **Thickness** | 6 in (15 cm) — accommodates double-sided sign faces |
| **Frame Construction** | Welded steel tube frame (2 in x 3 in x 1/8 in rectangular HSS tube) forming the arch shape and internal grid. This provides rigidity and wind resistance. The steel frame bolts to steel plates lag-bolted into the main pillar cap structure |
| **Sign Face Substrate** | 3/4-inch MDO (Medium Density Overlay) plywood — both front and back faces. MDO is the industry standard for painted sign faces: smooth, weather-resistant, holds paint beautifully |
| **Edge Banding** | 1x4 Western Red Cedar trim around the entire arch perimeter, routed with a bullnose profile |

### 4.2 Wind Load Considerations

The sign panel at 20 ft elevation will experience significant wind load. The steel tube frame is critical. Alberta Building Code requires structures to withstand wind loads of approximately 0.5 kPa (10 psf) for this region. The steel frame and bolted connections to the main pillars must be engineered to handle this. **Recommendation:** Have a local structural engineer review the connection detail between the sign panel frame and the pillar tops before construction.

### 5.3 Lettering and Graphics Options

Three options are presented in order of cost (low to high) and quality (good to best):

**Option A — Painted Lettering (Budget: $800-$1,500)**

Lettering is hand-painted or stencil-painted directly onto the MDO sign face using 1-Shot sign enamel paint in gold. A professional sign painter would lay out the lettering using vinyl stencil masks (cut on a plotter from the approved digital artwork) and spray or brush the gold enamel. The silhouette scene would be painted the same way. This is the most traditional and cost-effective approach. Touch-up is easy.

**Option B — Vinyl Graphics with Clear Overlaminate (Budget: $1,200-$2,500)**

Lettering and the silhouette scene are produced as cut vinyl graphics using 3M Scotchcal 7725 or Oracal 951 premium cast vinyl in metallic gold. Applied directly to the painted MDO surface. A clear UV overlaminate (3M 8519) is applied over the vinyl for additional weather protection. This provides very crisp, consistent lettering and is easier to replace individual elements if damaged. Lifespan: 7-10 years for premium cast vinyl.

**Option C — Dimensional Raised Lettering (Budget: $3,000-$6,000)**

Letters are CNC-routed from 1.5-inch thick HDU (High Density Urethane) foam board — the industry standard for dimensional sign letters. Each letter is individually shaped, primed, painted gold, and pin-mounted (with stainless steel pins) standing off the sign face by 1 inch. This creates real shadows and a premium 3D effect. The silhouette scene is also CNC-routed from HDU as a single panel and pin-mounted. HDU is lightweight, weather-proof, does not rot, and holds paint extremely well. **This is the recommended option for the premium look shown in the mock-ups.**

### 5.4 Back Side Lettering

The back side ("See You Soon, Neighbour!" etc.) uses the same lettering method as the front. If using Option C (dimensional), the script lettering for "See You Soon, Neighbour!" can be CNC-routed as connected script from HDU, creating a beautiful flowing dimensional effect.

## 10. CONSTRUCTION SEQUENCE

### Phase 1: Preparation (Day 1)

1. Remove all existing signage boards, brackets, and hardware from the current timber gateway structure.
2. Inspect existing timber posts for structural integrity — check for rot at the base, insect damage, and overall soundness. If any post is compromised, sister a new pressure-treated post alongside it before cladding.
3. Remove existing "COME AGAIN" and category signs. Remove ATV Operator notices (relocate to a separate post if needed).
4. Clean and prep existing metal gates — wire brush, sand, and prime.

### Phase 2: Secondary Pillar Footings (Day 1-2)

5. Mark locations for the two secondary pillars — each positioned on the outer side of the existing main posts, with a 2-inch gap.
6. Dig or auger holes 12 inches diameter x 48 inches deep (below frost line for Parkland County).
7. Set sonotubes, pour concrete, and install post anchor brackets. Level and plumb. Allow 24-48 hours to cure.

### Phase 3: Pillar Cladding (Day 2-3)

8. Build the cladding frame around each existing main post: attach 2x6 nailers horizontally at top, middle, and bottom of each post face using lag bolts into the existing timber.
9. Install vertical cladding boards (1x8 T&G cedar) or MDO plywood panels onto the nailer frame, creating the 24x24-inch square pillar appearance.
10. Build the flared base skirt: frame with 2x6, clad with matching material, creating the 30x30-inch base.
11. Build the pillar cap: frame with 2x8, create pyramid shape, install copper flashing or paint to match.
12. Install the secondary pillar: set 4x4 post into anchor bracket, plumb, and brace. Build cladding frame and install cladding to create 18x18-inch square appearance. Build matching base skirt and pyramid cap.
13. Install horizontal timber tie and diagonal brace between main and secondary pillars (hidden behind cladding at 8 ft level).

### Phase 4: Sign Panel Frame (Day 3-4)

14. Have the steel arch frame fabricated by a local welding shop per the dimensions in Section 4. The frame should arrive as one piece or two halves that bolt together at the center.
15. With a crew of 3-4 people and/or a small crane or telehandler, lift the steel arch frame into position on top of the pillar caps.
16. Bolt the steel frame to the connection plates that are lag-bolted into the pillar cap structure. Use minimum 1/2-inch lag bolts, 8 inches long, into solid wood.
17. Check level and plumb. Adjust as needed before final tightening.

### Phase 5: Sign Panel Faces (Day 4-5)

18. Cut MDO plywood panels to fit the arch frame — this will require multiple panels joined together. Use a jigsaw or router to cut the arch curve following the steel frame.
19. Attach MDO panels to the steel frame using self-tapping screws through the MDO into the steel tubes. Pre-drill to avoid splitting.
20. Install front face panels and back face panels.
21. Install 1x4 cedar edge trim around the entire arch perimeter using stainless steel finish nails and construction adhesive.
22. Fill all screw holes, seams, and joints with exterior wood filler. Sand smooth.

### Phase 6: Paint (Day 5-6)

23. Prime all surfaces (pillars, sign panel, trim) with Zinsser Cover Stain.
24. Apply first coat of Kokanee Green (Benjamin Moore Aura Exterior).
25. Apply second coat of Kokanee Green.
26. Paint the gold border frame line on both sign faces using painter's tape for crisp edges.
27. Apply lettering and graphics per chosen option (A, B, or C).
28. Apply clear topcoat (Sikkens Cetol SRD) over sign faces.
29. Paint refurbished gates in matching Kokanee Green.

### Phase 7: Lighting and Final Details (Day 6)

30. Install solar post cap lights on all four pillar caps.
31. Install optional solar uplights if desired.
32. Rehang refurbished and painted gates.
33. Touch up any paint damage from construction.
34. Clean up site, remove construction debris.
35. Final inspection — check all connections, verify clearance height (12 ft minimum), verify sign is level and plumb.

## 13. IMPORTANT NOTES

1. **No Permit Required:** This project is a renovation/cladding of an existing structure in its existing location. No new structure is being erected closer to the highway. Verify with Parkland County that a development permit is not required for sign face changes on existing structures.

2. **Structural Engineering:** The overhead sign panel at 12-20 ft elevation will experience significant wind loads. It is strongly recommended to have a local structural engineer review the steel frame design and the connection details to the pillar tops before fabrication.

3. **Utility Clearance:** Verify there are no overhead power lines or utility easements in the immediate area of the sign that could be affected by the increased height.

4. **Frost Depth:** Parkland County frost depth is approximately 4 ft (1.2 m). All new footings (secondary pillars) must extend to this depth minimum.

5. **Existing Structure Integrity:** Before cladding, thoroughly inspect the existing timber posts. If any post shows more than 25% cross-section loss due to rot or damage, it should be reinforced or replaced before proceeding.
