## Executive Summary

**BLUE SEAL** is an integrity certification and trust mark for businesses and organizations operating under the principles of Social Impact Capitalism. It functions as a verified integrity marker — similar in concept to the Certified B Corporation mark — awarded to businesses that have embedded social impact principles, ethical business practices, and community accountability standards into their operations.

The mark is being pursued as a **Canadian Certification Mark** under the Trademarks Act (section 2), as it is intended to be licensed to qualifying businesses rather than used exclusively by the applicant.

## BLUE SEAL — Full Trademark Service Description

### Class 35 — Business Management, Consulting, and Certification Services

Consulting services in the field of socially responsible and ethical business practices; business consulting services, namely, advising businesses and organizations on the adoption and implementation of social impact standards, ethical operational practices, and community accountability frameworks; providing business assessments and audits to evaluate conformity with defined standards of business integrity, social impact, and ethical operations; promoting public interest and awareness of socially responsible business practices by identifying and recognizing businesses that meet defined standards of integrity and social impact; administration of a business certification program, namely, evaluating and certifying businesses and organizations that meet defined standards relating to ethical business practices, social impact, community reinvestment, and data stewardship; business information services, namely, providing information to consumers and the public regarding the social impact, ethical practices, and integrity standards of certified businesses and organizations; compilation of business directories featuring businesses and organizations that have been evaluated and certified as meeting defined standards of integrity and social impact.

### Class 36 — Financial and Social Impact Services

Advisory services in the field of social impact investing, community reinvestment, and ethical financial practices; financial consulting services, namely, advising businesses and organizations on the integration of community reinvestment commitments and social impact funding models into their operations; administration of grant and funding programs for community development and social impact initiatives.

### Class 42 — Testing, Evaluation, and Certification Services

Testing, analysis, and evaluation of the business practices and operations of others to determine conformity with defined certification standards in the fields of business integrity, social impact, ethical operations, and data stewardship; quality assurance services, namely, inspection and evaluation of businesses and organizations to determine conformity with defined standards and practices in the fields of socially responsible business operations, community accountability, and ethical data governance; providing information and consultation in the field of business integrity certification standards.

### Class 41 — Educational Services

Educational services, namely, conducting workshops, seminars, and training programs in the fields of social impact business practices, ethical business operations, community accountability, and socially responsible entrepreneurship; providing educational resources and information in the field of social impact capitalism and ethical business practices.

## Certification Mark Consideration

Tim's note to trademark counsel (Jay Haugen):

> "Given that the BLUE SEAL is fundamentally a certification of integrity — a mark that businesses earn by meeting our defined standards — I would like your advice on whether it should be filed as a certification mark under the Trademarks Act rather than as an ordinary trademark. The mark will be licensed to businesses that meet our standards, and it is intended to distinguish businesses that operate with verified integrity from those that do not. This seems to align with the definition of a certification mark under section 2 of the Act. I would appreciate your guidance on which route gives us the strongest protection."

**Counsel's Response (Jay Haugen, March 12, 2026):**

> "For BLUE SEAL, this may be properly classified as a Certification Mark, which has additional requirements to meet when registering such marks. I will review the current requirements to be met but, generally speaking, we will need to submit information regarding your standards and qualifications other businesses need to meet in order to qualify to use the BLUE SEAL mark. Do you have a published set of rules, bylaws, requirements, etc. that set out what is required to qualify for BLUE SEAL status?"

**Action Required:** Develop and publish a formal set of BLUE SEAL standards, bylaws, and qualification requirements for submission to CIPO.

## Strategic Context

BLUE SEAL is positioned as part of the broader **Social Impact Capitalism (SIC)** philosophy developed by the SIC HB1000 Solve Team. It is designed to:

1. **Create a trust signal** for consumers and communities identifying businesses that operate with verified integrity
2. **Incentivize ethical business behaviour** by providing a recognized, earned certification
3. **Support Ruby Red clients** by ensuring that businesses serving the working poor, unbanked, and underbanked are held to a higher standard of community accountability
4. **Complement DO GOOD. GIVE BACK.** — the existing approved trademark — while serving a distinct certification function

The mark builds on the principle that **"it's expensive to be poor"** and that systemic change requires holding businesses accountable through verified standards, not just voluntary commitments.
