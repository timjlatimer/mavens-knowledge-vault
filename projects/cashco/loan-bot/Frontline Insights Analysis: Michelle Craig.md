# Frontline Insights Analysis: Michelle Craig

**Translating Human Expertise into Guardian Behaviors**

---

## Executive Summary

This document analyzes the exceptional feedback provided by Michelle Craig, Internal Compliance Auditor and a former frontline expert. Her answers provide a treasure trove of actionable insights that can be directly translated into specific behaviors and features for the Maven + Cashco Guardian. The key theme is a shift from passive support to **proactive, intelligent, and deeply human allyship**. Michelle's feedback gives us the source code for the Guardian's empathy and resourcefulness.

---

## Key Insights & Corresponding Guardian Behaviors

### 1. The "Split Funding" Strategy

*   **Insight (Q1)**: For clients who struggle with managing a lump sum (e.g., spending it too quickly at bingo or on family needs), splitting the loan into part cash and part a tucked-away cheque provides a crucial budgeting safety net and a sense of relief.

*   **Guardian Behavior**: The Guardian will be programmed to identify this potential need and proactively offer a split-funding solution during the loan setup.

    > **Guardian Script Idea**: *"I am so thrilled we got you approved! Now, let's make this money work for you all month long. I have an idea that has really helped others: we can give you some of it as cash now for your immediate needs, and put the rest on a separate, post-dated cheque or transfer. That way, you have a guaranteed safety net for later. How does that sound as a plan?"*

### 2. The "Empathy First" Protocol

*   **Insight (Q2)**: When a client is in distress about a missed payment, the first and most critical step is to validate their feelings ("I can't imagine how stressful that is") before immediately jumping to solutions or resources.

*   **Guardian Behavior**: This becomes a non-negotiable first step in the Guardian's "Struggle" protocol. The bot will be hardwired to lead with empathy.

    > **Guardian Script Idea**: *"Hey [Client Name], I saw the payment didn't go through. Before we talk about anything else, I just want to say that I can only imagine how stressful this must feel. Please don't worry about the payment right now. Let's just talk. What's going on in your life?"*

### 3. The "One Thing" Memory System

*   **Insight (Q5, Q6, Q9)**: The single most impactful trust-building technique is to remember one specific, important, non-financial detail about a client (a child's hockey tournament, a spouse's name, a new granddaughter) and reference it in future conversations. This makes the client feel seen and valued as a person.

*   **Guardian Behavior**: This is a primary directive for the **Life Ledger**. The Guardian will be programmed to identify and tag these "golden nuggets" of personal information and create reminders to bring them up organically.

    > **Guardian Script Idea**: *"I was just thinking about you and your family. How did your son's hockey team do at that big tournament in Calgary? I bet he had a blast."*

### 4. The "Community Connector" Database

*   **Insight (Q8)**: Frontline staff are a vital hub of local knowledge, connecting clients to non-financial resources like food banks (e.g., Rock Soup in Wetaskiwin) in a non-judgmental and culturally sensitive way. This is an everyday occurrence.

*   **Guardian Behavior**: The Guardian's **Program Coordinator** role must be populated with a dynamic, location-specific database of these non-judgmental community resources. The Guardian must be able to explain *why* a resource is a good fit (e.g., "it's set up like a grocery store, no ID needed").

    > **Guardian Script Idea**: *"I know things are tight right now, and I want to help. I found a local place called Rock Soup that sounds amazing. It's a food bank, but it's structured just like a grocery store, so you can shop for what you need with dignity. They are also fantastic at connecting people to Indigenous cultural resources. Here is their address. It might be a real help."*

### 5. The "Superpower" Feature Roadmap

*   **Insight (Q10)**: Michelle's ideas for the Guardian's superpowers are brilliant, practical, and perfectly aligned with the mission. They provide a clear roadmap for indispensable features.

*   **Guardian Behaviors**: These three features should be prioritized for development:

    1.  **The Meal & Grocery Genius**: A feature that can generate a meal plan based on pantry contents (from a photo) and then find the best local grocery deals for the missing ingredients.
    2.  **The Weekly Spending Reviewer**: A simple, non-judgmental weekly recap of spending that includes congratulatory messages for positive actions (e.g., "Great job keeping your bank balance above $50!").
    3.  **The Proactive Deal Finder**: An alert system that combines knowledge of the user's regular purchases with local sales flyers and loyalty programs (e.g., "Tuesday is 10% off at Safeway," or "You can get 20x the points on diapers at Shoppers this week").
