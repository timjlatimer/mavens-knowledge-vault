Hello Jessie,

Here are my views.

 

 

1.The Unforgettable Client Story: Think about a client you’ll never forget. What made that relationship so special or memorable? Can you share a brief story?

I had a client in collections last year, he was going through divorce, and he would share about his legal meetings with his wife and lawyers. He said he was going through some serious tough time. He would make promise but change it as he had other lenders take money from his account(we weren't EFTing Ontario at that time)he always mentioned that we were the nicest collectors and he called me telling his divorce is finalized and he paid his loan in full. Divorce is not a good story but he said he is free now and was really happy.

 

2.The Toughest Conversation: When a client calls in distress because they can't make a payment, what are the first words you say, and what is your primary goal in that conversation (beyond collecting the payment)?

 

I just listen calmly and don't mention anything about loan/payment. Once they are done, I wait for correct moment, I mention a loan or let them know I can call back next time, and we can discuss more then. 

 

3.Beyond the Loan: What are the most common topics you talk about with clients that have nothing to do with their loan or finances? (e.g., family, hobbies, work, their community).

Mostly snow and weather. If the client is sharing only, then I ask questions to show my interest . Some people dont like to share too much.

 

4.Celebrating the Wins: How do you celebrate a client's success, whether it’s paying off their loan or just making a few on-time payments in a row? What does that sound like?

I thank them and tell them I appreciate their efforts.  if they pay out the loan and I tell them let us know if they need any help in the future.

 

5.Your Secret Weapon: What is one small, simple thing you do that seems to have the biggest positive impact on how a client feels? (e.g., remembering a pet’s name, asking about a recent trip).

My biggest is I let client do most of the talking. More listening.

 

6.The First Five Minutes: When you talk to a new client for the first time, what is the most important thing you do to build a connection and earn their trust right away?

I usually let them know what I am doing so that they don't feel like its taking forever.

Explaining them every  little thing as this is their first time dealing.

 

7.The "Not Yet" Moment: If you have to tell someone they aren't approved for a loan, how do you handle that conversation to ensure they still feel respected and hopeful, not rejected?

I just tell them by saying I am afraid or use Sorry about that. I blame it on the "system" and usually tell them to re-apply after 30 days. 

 

8.The Community Connector: Have you ever suggested a non-financial resource to a client (e.g., a community event, a local charity, a job fair)? What was the situation? 

In collection if client would say they are having a difficult time I have sent them shelter and free food places a couple of times. 

 

9.The Power of Remembering: What are 2-3 types of personal details you try to remember about your long-term clients? How do you use that information to strengthen the relationship?

When you are on call it's hard to remember the different client. But I do look for any FSL notes. Sometime client has the name Raymond but FSL says like to be called Ray. So I use that name.

 

10.If You Had a Superpower...: Imagine you had a perfect digital assistant (our Maven + Cashco Guardian!) that could help you care for your clients. What are three things you would have it do for them every day or every week to make their lives better?

1.                      Keep track of payments and make a budget 

2.                      positive affirmations

3.                      Ways they can save money(like look for coupons, any item that's on the Wishlist, do market research about the item and see if they can get a better price.
