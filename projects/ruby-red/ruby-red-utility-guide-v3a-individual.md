# Lower Your Utility Bill: Individual Action Guide
## For Ruby Red - When You Need to Act NOW

*Estimated savings: $20-300/month | Time required: 30-60 minutes*

---

## 📞 YOUR #1 RESOURCE: CALL 211 FIRST

Before you do anything else, **dial 2-1-1** (free, confidential, available 24/7). Tell them:

> "I need help lowering my utility bills. Can you connect me with local assistance programs?"

211 is run by United Way and connects you to **every local program** in your area - assistance funds, weatherization, emergency shutoff prevention, and more. One call maps your entire landscape of help.

**Can't call?** Visit [211.org](https://www.211.org) or text your ZIP code to 898-211.

---

## 📋 BEFORE YOU CALL YOUR UTILITY COMPANY

### Documentation Checklist
Have these ready before you pick up the phone:

- [ ] Your account number (on your bill)
- [ ] Your last 3 bills (know your average monthly cost)
- [ ] How long you've been a customer
- [ ] Your household size and income range (for assistance programs)
- [ ] Proof of income if available (pay stubs, tax return)
- [ ] A pen and paper to write down names and confirmation numbers
- [ ] Competitor rates if available (look up online before calling)

### Best Time to Call
**Tuesday through Thursday, 10am-2pm** tends to have shorter wait times and more experienced representatives. Avoid Mondays (backlog) and Fridays (skeleton crew).

## 🎯 THE CALL (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Introduce Yourself

> "Hi, my name is [NAME]. I've been a customer for [X years] and I'm looking at my budget carefully. I'd like to discuss options to lower my monthly bill. Can you help me with that?"

### Step 2: If They Ask Why

> "I'm a mom trying to make ends meet, and every dollar counts for my family right now."

### Step 3: Ask These Questions (In This Order)

1. **"What assistance programs do you have for customers in my situation?"**
   - This opens the door to programs they won't volunteer otherwise

2. **"Can you review my rate plan to see if there's a better option for my usage?"**
   - You may be on the wrong plan entirely

3. **"Are there any discounts I'm not currently receiving?"**
   - Loyalty, paperless, autopay, senior, veteran, medical

4. **"Do you offer budget billing to even out my payments?"**
   - Spreads costs across 12 months for predictability

5. **"Do you have on-bill financing for energy efficiency upgrades?"**
   - Free upgrades paid back through your bill savings - zero upfront cost

### Step 4: Programs to Request By Name

| Program | What It Does | Typical Savings | How to Apply |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|--------------|
| **LIHEAP** | Federal heating/cooling assistance | Up to $500/year | Through your state (seasonal windows - ask 211 for dates) |
| **WAP (Weatherization)** | Free home insulation and efficiency upgrades | $283/year average | Through local Community Action Agency |
| **Budget Billing** | Averages your bill across 12 months | Predictability | Ask your utility directly |
| **Low-Income Rate** | Reduced rate for qualifying households | 20-35% off | Ask your utility directly |
| **CARE/FERA** | California-specific discount programs | 20-35% off | If in CA, ask your utility |
| **Medical Baseline** | Extra allowance for medical equipment | Varies | Doctor's note required |
| **Paperless + AutoPay** | Electronic billing discounts | $5-10/month | Ask your utility directly |
| **Community Solar** | Subscribe to a solar farm - no rooftop needed | 10-20% off electric | Visit energysage.com or ask 211 |
| **On-Bill Financing** | Utility finances efficiency upgrades, repaid through savings | Varies | Ask your utility directly |

### Step 5: If They Say No

**Escalation Script:**
> "I understand. Before I go, can I speak with a supervisor? I want to make sure I've explored every option available to me."

**The Competitor Card:**
> "I've been looking at [competitor name] and their rates are lower. Is there anything you can do to keep me as a customer?"

**The Last Ask:**
> "I appreciate your time. What CAN you do for me today, even if it's small?"

## 🔄 IF YOU CAN'T NEGOTIATE YOURSELF

**Option A: Bill Negotiation Services**
Services like Billshark report a **90% success rate** and average savings of **$300/year** (source: CNBC Select, 2026). They take a percentage of your savings as their fee - you only pay if they save you money.

**Option B: Ask for Help** (See Version B - Village Guide)
Someone in your community may be willing to make the call for you. You don't have to do this alone.

---

## 🌐 ACCESSIBILITY OPTIONS

**If English isn't your first language:**
> When you call, say: "I need an interpreter for [language]." Utility companies are required to provide language assistance.

**If phone calls cause anxiety:**
Many utilities now offer chat, email, or in-person options. Check your utility's website for "Contact Us" alternatives. Some Community Action Agencies will make the call on your behalf.

**If you are deaf or hard of hearing:**
Use TTY/TDD services or the 711 Telecommunications Relay Service to connect with your utility company.

## 📝 AFTER THE CALL

- [ ] Set a calendar reminder to call again in 6 months
- [ ] Check your next bill to verify changes were applied
- [ ] Share what worked with someone who might need it too

## 🔧 FREE QUICK WINS (Do While You Wait)

These cost nothing and save immediately:

| Action | Estimated Monthly Savings |
|--------|--------------------------|
| Unplug chargers and small appliances when not in use | $5-10 |
| Switch to cold water for laundry | $5-15 |
| Lower water heater to 120°F | $5-10 |
| Replace one incandescent bulb with LED ($1 at dollar store) | $2-5 per bulb |
| Close vents in unused rooms | $5-15 |
| Use power strips and turn off at night | $5-10 |
