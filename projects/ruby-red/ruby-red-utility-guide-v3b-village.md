# Lower Your Utility Bill: Village Power Guide
## For Ruby Red - When Your Maven Community Has Your Back

*Estimated savings: $50-300/month | Impact: You + your neighbors*

---

## 🏘️ YOU ARE NOT ALONE

The difference between struggling alone and thriving together is **asking for help** and **offering help**. Your Maven village is full of wisdom giants, skill sharers, and neighbors who face the same challenges.

This guide shows you how to **multiply your power** through community.

---

## 🚪 DON'T HAVE A VILLAGE YET? START HERE

If you're isolated and don't have a community around you yet, these three steps will begin building one:

### Step 1: Find Your First Connection
Post on a local Facebook group, Nextdoor, or community board:
> "I'm trying to lower my utility bills and wondering if anyone else is working on the same thing. Would love to share tips."

You only need ONE person to respond. That's the start of your village.

### Step 2: Have One Conversation
Meet for coffee (or a phone call) and share:
- What you've tried
- What worked and what didn't
- What programs you've found

Two people sharing knowledge is already more powerful than one person searching alone.

### Step 3: Invite One More
Each of you invites one more person. Now you're four. Four people is enough to start every strategy in this guide.

> "A village doesn't start with a hundred people. It starts with two who decide to help each other."

## 📞 VILLAGE STRATEGY #0: Map Your Resources Together

### What It Is
Before anyone negotiates, one person calls **211** (free, 24/7, United Way) and maps ALL local assistance programs for the entire village.

### How to Do It
One volunteer calls 211 and asks:
> "I'm part of a community group. Can you help me understand ALL the utility assistance programs available in [city/county]? I want to share this information with my neighbors."

### What to Document in a Shared List

| Program | Who Qualifies | How to Apply | Deadline | Contact |
|---------|--------------|--------------|----------|---------|
| LIHEAP | Income-based | State agency | Seasonal | [phone] |
| WAP Weatherization | Income-based | Community Action Agency | Rolling | [phone] |
| Community Solar | Renters, low-income | energysage.com | Open | [link] |
| [Local program] | [criteria] | [method] | [date] | [contact] |

### The Village Multiplier
**Alone**: You spend 3 hours researching, miss half the programs
**With Village**: One person spends 1 hour, everyone benefits from complete information

## 🌟 VILLAGE STRATEGY #1: The Wisdom Giant Call

### What It Is
Before you call the utility company, ask your village: *"Who has done this successfully?"*

### How to Do It
Post in your Maven community:
> "I need to lower my utility bill. Has anyone successfully negotiated with [utility company name]? What worked for you?"

### What You'll Get
- Actual scripts that worked locally (not generic internet advice)
- Names of helpful representatives
- Programs you didn't know existed
- Someone who might coach you through the call
- Emotional support from people who understand

### The Village Multiplier
**Alone**: You guess what might work
**With Village**: You KNOW what works because someone already did it

## 🤝 VILLAGE STRATEGY #2: The Skill Swap

### What It Is
Trade your skills for someone else's. No money needed.

### How to Do It
If you hate making negotiation calls, find someone who doesn't. Post:
> "I need help calling my utility company to negotiate my bill. I'm not great at phone negotiations. In exchange, I can offer [babysitting/cooking a meal/yard work/tutoring/etc.]. Anyone willing to trade?"

### Example Swaps

| You Offer | You Get |
|-----------|---------|
| 2 hours babysitting | Someone makes your utility call |
| Home-cooked meal | Help filling out assistance applications |
| Yard work | Ride to the utility office |
| Tech help | Budget coaching |
| Tutoring | Help with paperwork in English |

### Accessibility Note
This strategy is especially powerful for village members who face language barriers, phone anxiety, or hearing difficulties. The village fills the gap that institutions ignore.

### The Village Multiplier
**Alone**: You struggle with something you're bad at
**With Village**: You leverage what you're GOOD at

## 📚 VILLAGE STRATEGY #3: The Knowledge Cascade

### What It Is
One person learns, then teaches everyone else.

### How to Do It

**Option A - Be the Learner:**
Volunteer to become the village's "Utility Bill Expert":
- Call 211 and map all local programs
- Make the negotiation calls, take detailed notes
- Host a 30-minute teaching session (in person or Zoom)
- Create and maintain a shared document for the village

**Option B - Find the Expert:**
Ask in your village:
> "Does anyone know a lot about utility assistance programs? Would you be willing to share what you know with the group?"

### What to Include in the Village Knowledge Base
- [ ] All assistance programs available locally (from 211 call)
- [ ] Income limits and eligibility for each program
- [ ] Application deadlines (especially seasonal ones like LIHEAP)
- [ ] Best times to call (Tue-Thu, 10am-2pm)
- [ ] Names of helpful representatives at each utility
- [ ] Scripts that actually worked locally
- [ ] Community solar options in your area
- [ ] On-bill financing availability

### The Village Multiplier
**Alone**: You spend 5 hours researching
**With Village**: You spend 30 minutes learning from someone who already did

## 💪 VILLAGE STRATEGY #4: Collective Action

### What It Is
When many voices speak together, companies listen differently.

### How to Do It
Organize a "Utility Bill Action Week" in your village:

**Week Before:**
- Recruit 10-50 households to participate
- Share the negotiation script from Version A
- Create a shared spreadsheet to track results
- Assign a "buddy" to each person who needs support

**During the Week:**
- Everyone calls the same utility company
- Everyone mentions: "I'm part of a community group and we're all reviewing our options this week"
- Document every response - what worked, what didn't, who was helpful

**After the Week:**
- Compile results into the village knowledge base
- Calculate total collective savings
- Celebrate wins publicly (even small ones)
- If success rate is low, escalate collectively (see Strategy #6)

### The Village Multiplier
**Alone**: One complaint, easily ignored
**With Village**: Fifty calls in one week, impossible to ignore

## 🛡️ VILLAGE STRATEGY #5: The Emergency Shield

### What It Is
A village safety net for utility emergencies.

### The Maven Utility Fund (With Safeguards)

**Structure matters.** Informal money pooling can go wrong without clear rules. Here's how to do it right:

**Governance Rules:**
- Elect 3 fund managers (rotated annually)
- All transactions documented in a shared ledger
- Two signatures required for any disbursement over $50
- Monthly transparency report shared with all members
- Clear eligibility criteria agreed upon by the group

**How It Works:**
- Members contribute $5-10/month when they can (no shame for months you can't)
- Emergency draws available for utility shutoff prevention
- Repayment is flexible, interest-free, with a suggested timeline
- Maximum draw amount agreed upon by the group

**The Rapid Response Network:**
- Village members agree to share emergency alerts
- When someone faces shutoff, the village mobilizes
- Collective small contributions prevent disaster
- $10 from 20 people = $200 in 24 hours

**Legal Note:** For larger funds, consider partnering with a local credit union or nonprofit to provide fiscal sponsorship and accountability.

### The Village Multiplier
**Alone**: One emergency can spiral into disaster
**With Village**: Emergencies are absorbed by the community

## 🔥 VILLAGE STRATEGY #6: The Advocacy Amplifier

### What It Is
Turn individual problems into collective action.

### When to Use It
When you discover unfair billing practices, denied assistance despite qualifying, discriminatory treatment, or hidden fees affecting everyone.

### How to Do It
1. **Document** your experience in detail (dates, names, what was said)
2. **Share** with the village: "Is this happening to anyone else?"
3. **Collect** similar stories (3+ stories = a pattern)
4. **Escalate** collectively:

| Action | When to Use | How |
|--------|-------------|-----|
| Group complaint to Public Utilities Commission | Pattern of unfair practices | File jointly, reference all cases |
| Contact local news | Company refuses to address pattern | Tip line with documented stories |
| Attend utility commission meetings | Rate increases or policy changes | Show up as a group, testify |
| Contact elected officials | Systemic issues | Letters from multiple households |
| Social media campaign | Company ignores formal channels | Coordinated, factual, documented |

### The Village Multiplier
**Alone**: Your complaint is a data point
**With Village**: Your complaint is a movement

## 🏗️ VILLAGE STRATEGY #7: The Efficiency Co-op

### What It Is
Pool resources to reduce everyone's bills permanently.

### Bulk Buying (Save 30-50%)

| Item | Individual Cost | Bulk Cost (10 homes) | Savings |
|------|----------------|---------------------|---------|
| Weatherstripping kit | $15 | $8 each | 47% |
| LED bulbs (10-pack) | $12 | $7 each | 42% |
| Programmable thermostat | $25 | $15 each | 40% |
| Window insulation kit | $20 | $12 each | 40% |

### Tool Library
Instead of everyone buying tools, share them:
- Caulk guns, thermal leak detectors, power strips with timers
- Programmable thermostats (buy one, learn to program, pass it on)
- Kill-A-Watt meters (find which appliances are energy vampires)

### Work Parties
Saturday morning weatherization events:
- Experienced members teach newcomers
- Kids can help with simple tasks
- Potluck lunch after
- Rotate whose house gets worked on each month

### Community Solar Enrollment
- Research community solar options together (energysage.com)
- Bulk enrollment may qualify for better rates
- Even renters can participate - no rooftop needed
- Typical savings: 10-20% on electric bills

### The Village Multiplier
**Alone**: $200 for supplies, hours of solo work
**With Village**: $40 for supplies, fun community event, permanent savings
