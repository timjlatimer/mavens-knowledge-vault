## Part I: The Scoring Framework

### 1.1 Scoring Dimensions

Each initiative is scored on five dimensions, each rated 1–5:

| Dimension | What It Measures | 1 = Poor Fit | 5 = Excellent Fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Operational Fit (OF)** | How well Polsia's autonomous agents (engineering, marketing, outreach, support) match what this initiative actually needs to operate | Polsia can do almost nothing useful here | Polsia could run most of this initiative's operations |
| **Launch Readiness (LR)** | How ready the initiative is to hand operational tasks to an autonomous system — concept clarity, defined product, existing assets | Concept only, no product defined | Clear product, defined workflows, ready to hand off |
| **Ruby Red Alignment (RRA)** | How safely Polsia can serve this initiative's North Star — does autonomous operation risk harm to vulnerable people? | High risk of harm or misalignment | Fully safe, enhances mission delivery |
| **Revenue Potential (RP)** | How likely Polsia's model (subscription + 20% revenue share) generates meaningful return to fund the mission | No revenue pathway | Clear, near-term revenue pathway |
| **Speed to Value (SV)** | How quickly a Polsia deployment could produce observable, meaningful results | 12+ months | Results within 30 days |

**Total Score = Sum of 5 dimensions (max 25). Tier classification:**

| Score Range | Tier | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| 20–25 | **Tier 1 — Prime Pilot** | Launch immediately |
| 15–19 | **Tier 2 — Strong Candidate** | Plan and sequence |
| 10–14 | **Tier 3 — Conditional Fit** | Requires groundwork first |
| 5–9 | **Tier 4 — Not Yet** | Polsia is premature for this |
| 1–4 | **Tier 5 — Wrong Tool** | Polsia is structurally misaligned |

### 1.2 What Polsia Does Today (Capabilities Reference)

Before scoring, it is important to be precise about what Polsia's autonomous agents can actually do as of March 2026:

- **Engineering Agent:** Writes code, fixes bugs, deploys web applications, manages GitHub, maintains databases. Best for SaaS-style web products.
- **Marketing Agent:** Posts to Twitter/X, runs Meta ad campaigns, creates AI-generated UGC video ads via Sora, manages ad spend optimization.
- **Outreach Agent:** Researches prospects, verifies emails, sends personalized cold emails at scale.
- **Support Agent:** Responds to customer emails, handles basic support tickets, can issue refunds via Stripe.
- **CEO Agent:** Nightly strategic planning, priority-setting, daily status reports to the human founder.
- **PM Agent:** Triages feature requests, prioritizes bugs, maintains roadmap.

**What Polsia cannot do today:** Compliance-regulated financial services, in-person service delivery, community trust-building, empathy-first human relationships, regulated health or legal advice, complex government benefit navigation, or anything requiring licensed professional judgment.

## Part IV: The Pilot Recommendation — Kokanee Springs RV Park

### 4.1 Why Kokanee Springs

Kokanee Springs RV Park scores **20/25** — the only Tier 1 initiative in the portfolio. Here is the honest case for why it is the right first Polsia pilot:

**It is an active, operating business.** The lease agreement is being negotiated right now. There are real guests, real bookings, and real marketing needs. Polsia does not need to create a market — it needs to serve one that already exists.

**It has zero regulatory complexity.** Unlike CashCo, Pawn Princess, or Lady Loans, an RV park has no financial services licensing requirements, no compliance obligations that would conflict with Polsia's current capabilities, and no vulnerable population risk from autonomous communications.

**The Ruby Red risk is low.** Kokanee Springs serves families and tourists broadly. Autonomous marketing communications to potential RV park guests carry no meaningful harm risk. This is the cleanest possible environment to learn how Polsia operates.

**The speed to value is the fastest in the portfolio.** Polsia could have a Kokanee Springs website, social media presence, and booking outreach running within 7–14 days. This is faster than any other initiative.

**It generates real, measurable revenue.** Hospitality bookings are trackable. Polsia's 20% revenue share on incremental bookings driven by Polsia's marketing creates a clear, measurable ROI. The team will know within 30 days whether Polsia is delivering value.

**It is a learning laboratory.** Everything the team learns about how Polsia operates — its decision-making, its content quality, its outreach effectiveness, its limitations — can be applied to higher-stakes initiatives without risking Ruby Red's wellbeing.

## Part V: Tim's Honest Assessment — The Straight Talk

Tim asked for honesty. Here it is.

### 5.1 The Good News

Polsia is real. It is not vaporware. Ben Cera is running 1,468 companies with zero employees and $1.4M ARR. The technology works. The 80/20 model is sound. And the SIC portfolio has multiple initiatives that are genuinely well-suited for Polsia's capabilities.

The team's superpower — empathy, wisdom-giant expertise, mission alignment — is exactly the 20% that Polsia cannot replace. The team IS the 20%. Polsia handles the 80% of operational grind. This is a natural partnership.

### 5.2 The Honest Concerns

**The Ruby Red risk is real and must be managed.** The most important initiatives in the portfolio — Maven, Grace, NMM, Big Mama's Genius Village — are also the ones where autonomous AI poses the greatest risk of harm. Polsia's generic agents cannot provide trauma-informed, empathy-first interactions with financially vulnerable people. The team must be disciplined about where Polsia operates and where humans must remain in the loop.

**The portfolio is too early-stage for most Polsia deployments.** Of the 32 initiatives scored, only 1 (Kokanee Springs) is a Tier 1 candidate. Twelve are Tier 2 — strong candidates but requiring groundwork. Fourteen are Tier 3 or below. The honest reality is that many SIC initiatives are still in the concept stage, and Polsia cannot build what has not yet been defined. The team's most urgent priority is not Polsia — it is getting the initiatives defined clearly enough that Polsia can help.

**The 20% revenue share model needs negotiation.** Polsia takes 20% of all revenue generated through the platform. For social enterprises with thin margins and a 10% community tithe commitment, this is a significant cut. Before scaling Polsia across the portfolio, Tim should have a direct conversation with Ben Cera about a social enterprise pricing model. Given Polsia's stated interest in partnerships and its early-stage openness to collaboration, a reduced revenue share for mission-driven deployments is worth pursuing.

**Compliance is the ceiling.** Polsia has no SOC2, HIPAA, or financial services compliance. For the financial services cluster (CashCo, Pawn Princess, Lady Loans, NMM), this is a hard ceiling on what Polsia can do. The team should not wait for Polsia to solve this — it should build the compliance infrastructure independently and use Polsia only for the non-regulated layers.

**Quality control is non-negotiable.** Polsia's own name acknowledges the "AI slop" problem. The team must establish a content review protocol — grounded in the Jolene Protocol — before any Polsia-generated content reaches Ruby Red or the community. The 20% of taste includes quality control, and it cannot be delegated.

### 5.3 The Strategic Sequencing Recommendation

Based on the scoring, the recommended sequencing for Polsia deployment across the SIC portfolio is:

**Phase 1 (Now — 30 days):** Kokanee Springs RV Park pilot. Learn the system. Validate the model. Generate measurable ROI.

**Phase 2 (30–90 days):** PTK (Promises To Keep) + Ghost Buster. Well-defined SaaS product, clear B2B customer, no regulatory complexity. Build and launch with Polsia. Use the revenue to fund Phase 3.

**Phase 3 (90–180 days):** Blue Seal + Starts Local (bundled). Trademark applications will be resolved by then. Build the certification platform with Polsia. Run the outreach to businesses. Use Blue Seal as the gateway to the SIC ecosystem.

**Phase 4 (180+ days):** Maven infrastructure layer. By this point, the team will have learned how Polsia operates, established quality control protocols, and built the trust to deploy Polsia in higher-stakes contexts. Use Polsia for Maven's web infrastructure, content marketing, and outreach — but keep Grace firmly in human hands.

**Never (without major Polsia evolution):** NMM, Grace/Big Mama personas, regulated financial services decisions.

### 5.4 The One-Line Summary

> Polsia is a powerful tool for the 80% of operational grind that the Solve Team should not be doing manually. Start with Kokanee Springs. Learn the system. Then deploy strategically across the portfolio — always keeping the empathy, the community trust, and the Ruby Red relationship in human hands.
