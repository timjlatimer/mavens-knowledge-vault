## What This Race Was About

This was the first learning loop race ever run on the Maven/Grace platform itself — not on the learning loop protocol, but on the actual product that serves Ruby Red. The goal was to take the scattered, undocumented state of the Maven/Grace platform and transform it into a coherent, measurable, Ruby Red-centered system with a clear path from first delight to financial improvement.

The North Star was locked before the start gun fired and never changed:

> Build a Maven/Grace platform that surprises and delights Ruby Red from the very first moment — frictionless, no forms, no barriers, a Trojan horse entry point that earns her trust before she knows what it is. From that first delight, the platform immediately begins improving her financial situation. The 90-day KPI is a 100% lift in her net financial position.

## What We Found (The Honest Truth)

The Maven/Grace platform has a paradox at its core: **the governance layer is mature and operational (7 of 7 agents built), while the core product layer that directly serves Ruby Red is largely unbuilt (1 of 8 core agents live)**. The infrastructure to ensure quality is excellent. The features that create quality for Ruby Red are mostly still on paper.

The most critical finding was that **Grace's relationship memory tables are completely empty** — 0 records across all 5 tables despite 245 users and 12 signup conversations. This means Grace cannot remember anyone. The Big Mama effect — the feeling of being known and valued that keeps Ruby Red coming back — is not functioning. This is the single biggest trust gap in the platform.

The Song Studio Trojan Horse entry point is already built and operational, which is the good news. The hardest part of the delight-first strategy is done. But the bridge from delight to financial improvement — the conversation where Grace transitions from "that was a beautiful song" to "I found something that could save you $29 this month" — was not documented or designed. That bridge is now designed and specified.
