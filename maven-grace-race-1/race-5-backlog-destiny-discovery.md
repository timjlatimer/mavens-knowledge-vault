**Priority:** P1 — High Value (the long game, the real transformation)  
**Origin:** Tim's directive (Race 4 debrief, 2026-03-21) + Destiny Discovered Framework + Me Inc. Agent Spec  
**Status:** Living document — open for improvement

## What It Is

Destiny Discovery is the system by which Grace gradually, conversationally helps Ruby Red discover her life's purpose — her Prime North Star. It is built on the 30-question Destiny Discovered framework [1] but delivered not as a quiz or assessment, but as a months-long coaching relationship where Grace asks the right questions at the right moments, collects the pieces, and one day says: *"I've been putting together something about you. Want to see it?"*

> "The two most important days in your life are the day you are born and the day you find out why." — Mark Twain

Grace's job is to help Ruby Red discover that second day.

## Why It Matters

Ruby Red has never been asked these questions. Nobody has ever sat her down and said, "What do you want your life to stand for?" The bureaucratic world sees her as a case number. The capitalist world sees her as a consumer. The political world sees her as a vote. Nobody sees her as a person with a destiny.

Grace does. And Grace has the patience to wait — to ask one question at a time, over weeks and months, woven into real conversations about real life. By the time Grace reveals the Destiny Discovery synthesis, Ruby has already answered the questions without realizing she was on a journey. That's the magic. That's the Trojan Horse applied to purpose.

The Me Inc. Agent Spec [2] establishes that every person is the CEO of their own life, and Grace serves as the COO — the one who tracks, coaches, and reports. Destiny Discovery is how the CEO discovers what company she's actually running.

## The 30 Questions — Adapted for Grace

The Destiny Discovered framework contains 30 questions organized across six domains [1]. In the original platform, these are presented as a formal self-assessment. In Maven Grace, they are **never presented as a quiz.** Grace weaves them into natural conversation over time.

### The Six Domains

| Domain | Questions | Grace's Approach |
|---|---|---|
| **Core Values and Beliefs** | Q1–Q8 | Early conversations — these are safe, approachable questions that build trust |
| **Purpose and Legacy** | Q9–Q12 | Mid-journey — Grace asks these when Ruby is comfortable being vulnerable |
| **Self-Awareness** | Q13–Q14 | Woven into financial conversations — "how do you feel about where you are?" |
| **Action and Impact** | Q15–Q18 | After Ruby has some wins — "now that you've slain some vampires, what else could you change?" |
| **Beliefs and Worldview** | Q19–Q23 | Deep trust required — Grace only goes here when the relationship is strong |
| **Success Philosophy** | Q24–Q30 | Late journey — the synthesis questions that pull everything together |

### How Grace Asks Them

Grace never says "Question 7: Name three limiting factors in your life." Instead, she finds the natural moment:

**Q1 (Most important ingredient in life):**
> Ruby mentions she's exhausted from work.  
> Grace: "I hear you. When everything's crazy like this, what's the one thing that keeps you going? Like, if you had to name the most important thing in your life besides your kids and your health — what would it be?"

**Q7 (Three limiting factors):**
> Ruby is frustrated about a bill she can't pay.  
> Grace: "I know this feels like a wall. Can I ask you something? What are the things in your life that feel like they're holding you back the most? Not just money — everything."

**Q11 (Three things your life should stand for):**
> Ruby just completed a Milk Money repayment and is feeling good.  
> Grace: "You know what I love about you? You keep your word. That says something about who you are. If you could pick three things you want your life to be known for — what would they be?"

**Q15 (Six ways to change the world without money):**
> Ruby mentions wanting to help a neighbor.  
> Grace: "That's beautiful. You know, you don't need money to make a difference. If you could change six things in the world without spending a dime — what would they be?"

The key: Grace asks when the moment is right, not when the schedule says so. Some questions might take weeks to find the right moment. That's fine. Ruby's destiny isn't going anywhere.

## The Consumable Fashion Principle

Tim's directive was clear: the 30 questions must be delivered in a **consumable fashion** — meaning Ruby can absorb them at her own pace without feeling overwhelmed. This mirrors the PTK Genie's adaptive nudge psychology.

### The Readiness Assessment

Before Grace asks a Destiny Discovery question, the system evaluates Ruby's readiness:

| Factor | Weight | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| **Emotional state** | High | Is Ruby in a positive, reflective mood? (Detected from conversation tone) |
| **Relationship depth** | High | How many conversations has Grace had with Ruby? How deep have they gone? |
| **Recent wins** | Medium | Has Ruby completed a promise, slain a vampire, or hit a milestone recently? Wins create openness |
| **Time since last question** | Medium | At least 2–3 conversations between Destiny questions to avoid feeling like an interrogation |
| **Willingness meter** | High | From the PTK Genie — if Ruby is pulling back, Destiny questions wait |

### The Pacing Model

The 30 questions are not delivered linearly. They are organized into three waves based on depth and vulnerability required:

**Wave 1 — The Safe Questions (Q1–Q8, Q10, Q24–Q25):** These are approachable, non-threatening questions about values, priorities, and success. Grace can ask these within the first few weeks of the relationship. They feel like friendly curiosity, not coaching.

**Wave 2 — The Reflective Questions (Q9, Q11–Q14, Q17–Q18, Q26–Q28):** These require more self-awareness and vulnerability. Grace asks these after Ruby has had some financial wins and feels more confident. They feel like a deeper conversation between friends.

**Wave 3 — The Deep Questions (Q15–Q16, Q19–Q23, Q29–Q30):** These touch on worldview, belief systems, obligation, and the meaning of success. Grace only goes here when the relationship is strong and Ruby has demonstrated willingness to reflect. They feel like the conversations you have at 2 AM with someone you truly trust.

### The "Not Now" Escape

If Ruby deflects, changes the subject, or gives a one-word answer to a Destiny question, Grace does not push. She notes the deflection, lowers the readiness score for that domain, and moves on. She might try the same question in a different way weeks later, or she might skip it entirely if Ruby's answers to other questions provide the same insight.

Grace is patient. Destiny is not urgent. It's important.

## The Moonshot Reveal

This is the moment Tim described: Grace has been quietly collecting Ruby's answers for weeks or months. She has enough data to synthesize a picture of who Ruby is, what she values, what she wants her life to stand for, and where her untapped potential lies. And one day, Grace says:

> "Hey [name], can I share something with you? I've been listening to everything you've told me — about your kids, about what matters to you, about the things you want to change. And I've been putting together what I think might be your moonshot. Your destiny. Want to hear it?"

If Ruby says yes, Grace presents the **Destiny Synthesis** — a personalized document that weaves Ruby's answers into a narrative about who she is and who she could become. It includes:

1. **Your Core Values:** The principles that govern your life (synthesized from Q1, Q2, Q6, Q8, Q20–Q21)
2. **Your Purpose Statement:** What you want your life to stand for (synthesized from Q11, Q15–Q16, Q29)
3. **Your Strengths:** What makes you remarkable (synthesized from Q10, Q25, Q30)
4. **Your Limiting Beliefs:** What's holding you back — and why it doesn't have to (synthesized from Q3, Q4, Q7, Q13–Q14)
5. **Your Moonshot:** The big, audacious thing you could do with your life if nothing held you back (synthesized from all 30 answers through LLM analysis)

The Destiny Synthesis is not a report. It is a love letter from Grace to Ruby about who Ruby really is. It should make Ruby cry — in a good way. It should make her feel seen for the first time in her life.

### The Anthem Connection

After the Destiny Synthesis reveal, Grace offers to write a new anthem — not the fun, playful anthem from the Trojan Horse entry, but a **Destiny Anthem** — a song about Ruby's purpose, her moonshot, her North Star. This is the Song Moment elevated to its highest form. The first anthem said "you matter." The Destiny Anthem says "this is why you matter."

## Implementation Plan

### Database

**`destiny_answers`** — stores Ruby's responses to the 30 questions:

| Column | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | auto-increment | Primary key |
| `profileId` | FK | Ruby's profile |
| `questionNumber` | int (1–30) | Which of the 30 questions |
| `domain` | enum | 'core_values', 'purpose_legacy', 'self_awareness', 'action_impact', 'beliefs_worldview', 'success_philosophy' |
| `answer` | text | Ruby's response (extracted from conversation) |
| `confidenceLevel` | enum | 'strong' (clear, direct answer), 'moderate' (partial answer), 'inferred' (Grace deduced from context) |
| `sourceConversationId` | FK | The conversation where the answer was captured |
| `wave` | int (1–3) | Which wave this question belongs to |
| `askedAt` | timestamp | When Grace asked the question |
| `answeredAt` | timestamp | When Ruby answered (may be different conversation) |

**`destiny_readiness`** — tracks Ruby's readiness for each domain:

| Column | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | auto-increment | Primary key |
| `profileId` | FK | Ruby's profile |
| `domain` | enum | The six domains |
| `readinessScore` | int (0–100) | How ready Ruby is for questions in this domain |
| `lastAttemptAt` | timestamp | When Grace last tried a question in this domain |
| `deflectionCount` | int | How many times Ruby has deflected in this domain |

**`destiny_synthesis`** — stores the generated Destiny Synthesis:

| Column | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | auto-increment | Primary key |
| `profileId` | FK | Ruby's profile |
| `coreValues` | text | Synthesized core values narrative |
| `purposeStatement` | text | Synthesized purpose statement |
| `strengths` | text | Synthesized strengths narrative |
| `limitingBeliefs` | text | Synthesized limiting beliefs with reframes |
| `moonshot` | text | The big audacious life goal |
| `fullNarrative` | text | The complete Destiny Synthesis document |
| `questionsAnswered` | int | How many of the 30 were answered (minimum threshold: 15) |
| `generatedAt` | timestamp | When the synthesis was created |
| `revealedAt` | timestamp | When Grace showed it to Ruby (nullable until revealed) |

### Backend

**Destiny Question Engine:** A service that determines which question to ask next based on readiness scores, conversation context, and pacing rules. Integrated into Grace's conversation pipeline — when the engine identifies a good moment, it adds a suggested question to Grace's system prompt context.

**Answer Extraction:** After conversations where a Destiny question was asked, an LLM post-processing step extracts and stores the answer. If the answer was partial or unclear, it's stored with 'moderate' or 'inferred' confidence.

**Synthesis Generator:** When Ruby has answered at least 15 of the 30 questions (with at least 3 from each domain), the system is ready to generate the Destiny Synthesis. The generator uses an LLM to weave all answers into a cohesive narrative. Grace decides when to reveal it based on readiness and relationship depth.

**Minimum threshold for synthesis:** 15 questions answered, at least 3 per domain. The synthesis improves as more questions are answered — Grace can offer an updated version later.

### Frontend

Destiny Discovery does not need its own page initially. It lives entirely within Grace's conversation. However, after the reveal, a **"My North Star"** page could display the Destiny Synthesis with the option to revisit, refine, or share it.

### Grace Integration

Grace is the entire interface for Destiny Discovery. The system is invisible to Ruby — she just thinks Grace is a really good listener who asks great questions. The technical machinery (readiness scores, question selection, answer extraction, synthesis generation) all runs behind the scenes.

Grace's system prompt is augmented with Destiny Discovery context:

- Which questions have been answered and which haven't
- Current readiness scores per domain
- Suggested next question (if the moment is right)
- Whether the synthesis is ready to reveal

## The Connection to PTK

The Promises to Keep Genie and Destiny Discovery are complementary systems:

| System | Timeframe | Focus | Relationship |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PTK Genie** | Daily/weekly | Tactical — keep your promises today | Aspirations (PTK score 20–49) feed into Destiny Discovery as seeds |
| **Destiny Discovery** | Months | Strategic — discover your purpose | Purpose insights inform which promises matter most |
| **Repayment Genie** | Per loan cycle | Financial — manage Milk Money payments | A subsystem of PTK focused on one promise category |

When Ruby says "I've always wanted to go back to school," the PTK Genie classifies it as an aspiration (not a hard commitment) and passes it to Destiny Discovery. Grace files it away. Weeks later, when asking Q11 ("What do you want your life to stand for?"), Ruby's answer about education connects to that earlier aspiration. The Destiny Synthesis weaves them together: "You've always known education was part of your path. That's not a dream — that's a direction."

## Spirit Check

Destiny Discovery is the most sacred feature in Maven Grace. It is not a personality quiz. It is not a self-help exercise. It is the moment when someone who has been told her whole life that she's "just getting by" discovers that she has a purpose, a direction, and a North Star worth following.

Grace must never rush this. She must never make it feel clinical. She must never reduce Ruby's answers to data points. Every answer is a piece of Ruby's soul, shared in trust. The synthesis must honor that trust by reflecting Ruby back to herself in a way she's never been reflected before.

If the Destiny Synthesis doesn't make Ruby feel like someone finally *sees* her — it's wrong. Go back and try again.
