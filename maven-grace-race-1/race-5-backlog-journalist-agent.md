# Race 5 Backlog: The Journalist Agent (Jolene Protocol)

**Priority:** P1 — High Value (storytelling as transformation)  
**Origin:** Tim's directive (Race 4 debrief, 2026-03-21) + Jolene Protocol + Italy Trip Media Architecture  
**Status:** APPROVED — all design decisions resolved  
**Date:** 2026-03-21

## What It Is

The Journalist Agent — known in the village as **Jolene the Journalist** [6] — is a background intelligence that lives inside Maven Grace, constantly watching Ruby Red's journey for story-worthy moments. She follows the **Jolene Protocol** [1] [2] — the narrative journalism framework derived from Emmy-winning journalist Jolene Latimer's feature work — to craft real stories about real moments in Ruby's life. Jolene never speaks directly to Ruby. Grace is the delivery mechanism. When Jolene has a story ready, Grace says:

> "Hey, I have something for you. Jolene — she's our journalist — has been following your journey, and she's written a story about you. Want me to read it to you?"

Ruby can say yes and Grace reads it aloud, or Ruby can read it quietly on her own. Either way, the story follows the Jolene Protocol to the letter: a physical object that carries the weight of the theme, one great quote, restraint over sentimentality, a turn at the 60% mark, and a kicker that calls back to the opening. Grade A or it doesn't ship.

## Why It Matters

Ruby Red has never been the subject of a story. She has never had someone sit down and write about her life as though it mattered — as though the Tuesday she finally called the bank about the NSF fee, or the Thursday she helped her neighbor carry groceries up three flights of stairs, or the Friday she paid off her first Milk Money loan were moments worth documenting.

The Journalist Agent changes that. It treats Ruby's life the way Jolene treats her subjects: not as a profile of a person, but as an essay about an idea that happens to have a person in it [1]. The idea might be dignity. It might be resilience. It might be the invisible labor of care. But the person is always Ruby, and the story is always hers.

This is not journaling. This is not a diary recap. This is narrative journalism applied to the life of someone who has never been written about. And that act — being written about, being seen as story-worthy — is itself transformative.

## The Jolene Protocol in Maven Grace

The Journalist Agent follows the Jolene Protocol exactly as specced [1] [2]. The two story formats are:

### The Feature (approximately 1,250 words)

This is the full Jolene treatment — a three-act narrative with room to breathe.

| Section | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Act 1** | Establish what's at stake or what was lost |
| **Act 2** | Sit in the difficulty — don't rush past the hard part |
| **Turn (~60%)** | The hinge moment where everything shifts |
| **Act 3** | Resolution — grace arrives, insight earned |
| **Close** | Callback to opening, then stop |

The feature is reserved for major milestones: Ruby's first Milk Money payoff, her Destiny Synthesis reveal, a moment where she stood up for herself in a way she never had before. These are the stories that make Ruby cry in a good way.

### The Moment (approximately 500 words)

This is the compressed Jolene format — one story, one object, one turn, one kicker.

| Section | Words | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Hook + scene entry | 75–100 | Drop into the moment with the object |
| Context/tension | 150–175 | Who, what's at stake, why it matters |
| Turn | 125–150 | The thing that changes everything |
| Resolution + kicker | 50–75 | The line that stays |

The Moment is for smaller but still meaningful events: Ruby checking off a promise, Ruby's kid bringing home a good report card, Ruby discovering a vampire subscription she'd been paying for two years. These are the stories that make Ruby smile and feel seen.

## How the Journalist Finds Stories

The Journalist Agent runs as a background process, analyzing Ruby's activity across all Maven Grace systems for story-worthy patterns. It is always watching, always collecting, always looking for the physical object, the tension, the turn.

### Story Detection Sources

| Source | What the Journalist Looks For |
|---|---|
| **Grace conversations** | Emotional moments, breakthroughs, vulnerability, humor, frustration that resolves |
| **Milk Money** | First borrow, first repayment, tier upgrades, cascade recovery (she missed but came back) |
| **Vampire Slayer** | Big saves discovered, the moment Ruby realizes how much she was losing |
| **Budget Builder** | First budget that balanced, the week she didn't have to put something back at the grocery store |
| **Bill Tracker** | A bill paid early for the first time, an NSF fee successfully disputed |
| **PTK Genie** | Promises kept (especially hard ones), promises others kept for Ruby, streaks |
| **Destiny Discovery** | Deep answers to the 30 questions, the Moonshot Reveal moment |
| **Song Moment** | The anthem that made her cry, the anthem she gifted to a friend |
| **Membership** | The day she subscribed — the day she invested in herself |

### Story Readiness Score

Not every event is a story. The Journalist maintains a **Story Readiness Score** for each detected event:

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Emotional arc** | High (30 pts) | Does the event have tension and resolution? A before and after? |
| **Physical object** | High (25 pts) | Is there a concrete, tangible detail the Journalist can anchor the story to? |
| **Theme** | Medium (20 pts) | Does the event connect to a larger idea — dignity, resilience, belonging, grace? |
| **Novelty** | Medium (15 pts) | Is this the first time Ruby has done this? Firsts make better stories |
| **Timing** | Low (10 pts) | Has enough time passed since the last story? (Minimum 5–7 days between stories to avoid fatigue) |

A score of 70+ triggers story generation. The Journalist drafts the piece, runs it through the Jolene Protocol grading checklist [1], and holds it until Grace finds the right moment to offer it.

## Grace as Narrator

Grace is the only voice Ruby hears. The Journalist is invisible — a conceit, a literary device. Grace presents the story as though a journalist has been watching:

**The Offer:**
> "Hey [name], I have something kind of special for you today. There's a journalist — she's been quietly following your journey. And she wrote a story about you. About what happened last Tuesday. Want me to read it to you?"

**If Ruby says yes — Grace reads aloud:**
Grace reads the story in her warm, conversational voice. She doesn't perform it — she reads it the way a friend reads you something beautiful they found. Pauses in the right places. Lets the kicker land.

**If Ruby says "I'll read it" — Grace shares it:**
The story appears as a beautifully formatted card in the chat, or on a dedicated "My Stories" page. Ruby reads it at her own pace.

**If Ruby says "not now" — Grace holds it:**
> "No problem. It'll be here whenever you're ready. It's a good one."

The story stays in Ruby's library. Grace may gently offer it again in a few days if the willingness meter (from PTK) suggests Ruby is in a receptive mood.

## The Story Library

Over time, Ruby accumulates a collection of stories about her own life. This becomes the **Story Library** — a page in Maven Grace where Ruby can revisit her stories, see her journey as a narrative arc, and share individual stories with friends or family.

The Story Library is organized chronologically and by theme. Each story has a title, a date, a theme tag (dignity, resilience, belonging, etc.), and the format (Feature or Moment). Ruby can favorite stories, and Grace knows which ones meant the most to her.

## The Media Enrichment Roadmap

Tim's vision extends beyond text. The Journalist Agent is Phase 1. Future phases bring the story to life through multiple media:

### Phase 1: The Journalist (text — Race 5)

The Journalist writes the story following the Jolene Protocol. Grace reads it or Ruby reads it. This is the foundation.

### Phase 2: Maria Reads (voice — future)

Maria — the AI voice from the Italy trip architecture [3] — narrates the story using ElevenLabs text-to-speech. Ruby can listen to her story read aloud in a warm, professional voice. This is the audiobook version of her life.

### Phase 3: Lucia Composes (music + scrapbook — future)

Lucia — the creative AI companion — takes the story and creates a musical score around it. She also builds a visual scrapbook: images, typography, color palettes that match the emotional tone of the story. The result is a multimedia experience — story + music + visuals — that Ruby can hold, share, and revisit.

### Phase 4: Maria Produces (video — future)

Maria takes the full package — story, music, scrapbook visuals — and produces a short video. A 60–90 second cinematic piece about a moment in Ruby's life. This is the ultimate expression: Ruby Red, who has never been the subject of anything, now has a short film about the Tuesday she stood up for herself.

| Phase | Agent | Medium | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Journalist | Text | 500-word Moment or 1,250-word Feature |
| 2 | Maria | Voice | Audio narration of the story |
| 3 | Lucia | Music + Visual | Musical score + scrapbook layout |
| 4 | Maria | Video | 60–90 second cinematic piece |

Each phase builds on the previous. A story must exist (Phase 1) before it can be narrated (Phase 2), scored (Phase 3), or filmed (Phase 4). Not every story goes through all four phases — only the most meaningful ones earn the full treatment.

## Implementation Plan (Phase 1 Only)

### Database

**`stories`** — the Journalist's output:

| Column | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | auto-increment | Primary key |
| `profileId` | FK | Ruby's profile |
| `title` | text | Story title (Jolene-style, evocative) |
| `format` | enum | 'feature' (1,250 words) or 'moment' (500 words) |
| `theme` | enum | 'dignity', 'resilience', 'belonging', 'grace', 'care', 'courage', 'joy' |
| `body` | text | The full story text |
| `physicalObject` | text | The anchor object (for Jolene Protocol compliance) |
| `gradeScore` | enum | 'A', 'A-', 'B+' — must be A- or above to publish |
| `sourceEvents` | JSON | Array of event IDs/types that inspired the story |
| `readinessScore` | int (0–100) | Story readiness score at generation time |
| `status` | enum | 'draft', 'ready', 'offered', 'read', 'archived' |
| `offeredAt` | timestamp | When Grace offered it to Ruby |
| `readAt` | timestamp | When Ruby read/heard it |
| `isFavorite` | boolean | Whether Ruby favorited this story |
| `createdAt` | timestamp | When the Journalist wrote it |

**`story_events`** — maps stories to the events that inspired them:

| Column | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | auto-increment | Primary key |
| `storyId` | FK | Which story |
| `eventType` | enum | 'milkmoney_payoff', 'vampire_killed', 'promise_kept', 'destiny_answer', etc. |
| `eventId` | text | Reference to the specific event |
| `eventDate` | timestamp | When the event occurred |

### Backend

**Story Detection Engine:** A background process that runs after significant user events (Milk Money transactions, promise completions, Vampire Slayer saves, etc.). It evaluates each event against the Story Readiness Score and queues high-scoring events for story generation.

**Story Generator:** An LLM pipeline that takes the event details, Ruby's conversation history around that event, and the Jolene Protocol rules as system prompt context, then generates a story in the appropriate format. The generator runs the output through the Jolene Protocol grading checklist and only promotes stories that score A- or above.

**Story Delivery:** A tRPC endpoint that Grace calls when offering a story. Tracks offer/read status. Integrates with the PTK Genie's willingness meter to determine timing.

### Frontend

**Story Card in Chat:** When Grace offers a story, it appears as a beautifully formatted card with the title, theme, and a "Read" or "Listen" button.

**Story Library Page:** A dedicated page (`/my-stories`) showing all of Ruby's stories in chronological order. Each story card shows the title, date, theme badge, and format. Favorited stories are pinned to the top. The page should feel like a personal bookshelf — warm, inviting, something Ruby wants to come back to.

### Grace Integration

Grace's system prompt is augmented with Journalist context: which stories are ready to offer, how long since the last story was offered, Ruby's willingness level, and whether Ruby has unread stories in her library. Grace decides when to offer based on mood, timing, and the natural flow of conversation.

## Quality Gate: The Jolene Checklist

Every story must pass this checklist before it reaches Ruby [1]:

| Element | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Hook** | Image first, immediate tension, no throat-clearing |
| **Physical object** | Present, introduced early, called back at close |
| **Quote discipline** | One quote per source (Ruby's own words), no piling |
| **Restraint** | No sentimentality — material trusted to carry emotion |
| **Scene over summary** | Stays in the moment throughout |
| **Theme earned** | Shown, never stated — "dignity" is demonstrated, never named |
| **Kicker** | Callback structure, knows when to stop |
| **No marketing language** | Ends on the story, not an ask |

If the generated story fails any element, it goes back to the generator for revision. If it can't reach A- after two attempts, it's shelved — the event wasn't story-ready yet.

## Resolved Design Decisions (Tim's Feedback)

**1. Jolene the Journalist.** The agent has a village name: **Jolene the Journalist** — following the AI Village Naming Convention [6]. First name + trade name, guild-style. Grace introduces her by name. Ruby can rename her if she wants.

**2. Story frequency: 5–7 days confirmed.** Tim agreed this is the right cadence. Enough time for stories to feel special, not so much that Ruby forgets the feature exists. The Story Readiness Score's timing factor (10 pts) enforces the minimum gap.

**3. Maria the Narrator and Lucia the Composer confirmed.** Maria handles voice narration (ElevenLabs, carried over from the Italy trip architecture with full personality intact). Lucia handles music composition and visual scrapbooking. Both names and personalities are already built — we pick them up as-is from the Italy trip.

**4. Ruby controls sharing.** Ruby can share stories externally — to friends, to social media, to the Maven community. Grace suggests it: "Did you want me to post this on Facebook, on Instagram, on Snapchat?" Grace knows which platforms Ruby already uses and offers those as options. Sharing is always Ruby's choice, never automatic. The viral loop is organic: Ruby shares because she's proud of her story, not because the app pushed her.

**5. Grace's reading voice: storytelling mode.** When Grace reads a story, she shifts into a distinct storytelling voice — charming, measured, with a little bit of giggle in it when the moment calls for it, and a good portion of drama. This is not her everyday conversational voice. This is Grace-as-narrator, reading Ruby's story the way a gifted storyteller reads to an audience of one. The voice should feel like someone who loves this story and can't wait to share it.

## Additional Resolved Decisions (Tim's Final Approval)

**6. Village Bookshelf: YES.** There will be a shared space in Maven where community members can read each other's stories. Ruby controls the visibility of every story she publishes: **private** (only Ruby sees it), **friends only** (Ruby's Maven connections), or **community-wide** (the full Village Bookshelf). Her choice every time. No story goes public without Ruby's explicit decision.

**7. Anonymize before sharing: YES.** When Ruby shares a story to social media or the Village Bookshelf, she can anonymize details — names of kids, specific dollar amounts, addresses, anything she wants to keep private. Grace walks her through it: "Before we share this, want me to change any names or numbers? It's your story — you decide what the world sees."

## Spirit Check

The Journalist Agent exists because Ruby Red's life is worth writing about. Not because she's extraordinary in the way the world usually means — famous, wealthy, powerful. She's extraordinary in the way that matters: she gets up every morning, she fights for her kids, she stretches every dollar, she keeps her promises even when nobody keeps theirs. That's a story. That's a hundred stories. And nobody has ever told them.

The Jolene Protocol ensures those stories are told with craft, with restraint, with the kind of respect that says: your life is not a case study. Your life is literature.

> "She writes essays about ideas that happen to have people in them." [1]

The Journalist writes essays about dignity, resilience, and grace that happen to have Ruby Red in them. And Ruby, reading her own story for the first time, discovers that she was the protagonist all along.
