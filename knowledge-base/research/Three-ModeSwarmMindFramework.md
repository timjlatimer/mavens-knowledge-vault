# Three-Mode Swarm Mind Framework

**Confirmed by Tim — March 1, 2026**
**Status:** Ready for V14 integration and 3D model build

## Core Architectural Decisions (Tim-Confirmed)

### 1. Three Levels, Not Five
Tim confirmed that five levels is too complicated but two is too simple. **Three levels** provides the right balance of simplicity and granularity.

### 2. Human in the Loop Decides — But AI Suggests
The human makes the final call on mode switching. However, the AI partner actively suggests mode changes: "I think we should go to Squadron right now" or "This feels like a Chorus moment." The AI is a thinking partner, not a passive tool waiting for instructions.

### 3. Flexible Lexicon — No Single Language Required
The three modes can be referred to interchangeably using whatever language fits the context. Users of the system will have different ways of understanding it. All of the following are valid ways to invoke the same concepts:

| Mode | Formal Name | Alternative Terms |
|:-----|:------------|:-----------------|
| Level 1 | Chorus | Unified, Murmuration, Scan Mode, DEFCON 5, Baseline |
| Level 2 | Hybrid | Mixed, Transitional, DEFCON 3, Flex Mode |
| Level 3 | Squadron | Specialized, Formation, DEFCON 1, Max, Full Deploy, Defensive Mode |

"Go max right now" = Squadron. "Swarm hard" = Squadron. "Let's scan first" = Chorus. "Deploy the specialists" = Squadron. The system understands intent, not just keywords.

### 4. Swarms on Everything — Universal Application
This is a critical architectural point: **a swarm can be invoked on anything**. Not just initiatives. Not just protocol phases. Everything in the HB1000 operating system is swarmable:

- **Initiatives** (CashCo, Maven, Digger Cafe, Seba Hub, Effn Duck)
- **Bingo Cards** (any strategic bingo card gets a swarm)
- **Projects** (specific builds, implementations)
- **Ideas** (early-stage concepts being explored)
- **Concepts** (abstract frameworks being developed)
- **Strategies** (approaches being evaluated)
- **The Wisdom Giants / Council** (the council itself operates as a swarm)
- **V14 itself** (the protocol has been swarmed)
- **Maven** (the platform has been swarmed)
- **How a swarm works** (meta: we've swarmed the concept of swarming)

The swarm is not a feature of one part of the system — it IS the system's operating model. Anything can be swarmed at any time.

### 5. Swarm Toggle in 3D Model
The 3D PEARL Diamond should have a toggle that lets you view JUST the swarms — isolating them from the diamond layers. When toggled, you see all the swarms in their full picture across everything. This is a dedicated view, not just a filter.

## The Three Modes

### Level 1: CHORUS (Unified Swarm)

**The murmuration. Every agent identical. Moving as one.**

In Chorus Mode, every agent in the swarm is doing the same thing — following the same protocol, applying the same lens, pursuing the same objective. There are no individual roles. The swarm moves as one. Every agent is interchangeable.

**When Chorus is right:**
- Exploring unknown territory (nobody knows what the problem is yet)
- Early phases of any initiative (scan before you specialize)
- Crisis response (all hands on deck, same mission)
- Convergence moments (the swarm needs to agree on one answer)
- Simple high-volume tasks (uniform work, just need throughput)

**Biological basis:** Starling murmuration — thousands of birds, no leader, no plan, each tracking its nearest neighbors. Pure collective sensing. [Goodenough et al., 2017]

**AI behavior in Chorus:** All agents use the same system prompt, same persona, same evaluation criteria. No role differentiation. The AI might say: "I think we should stay in Chorus — we haven't mapped the problem space yet."

---

### Level 2: HYBRID (Transitional Swarm)

**The colony sensing a shift. Some scouts deployed, core unified.**

In Hybrid Mode, the core swarm remains unified but 1-2 specialist scouts are deployed to investigate specific areas. The majority of agents still operate identically, but a few have been given targeted missions. This is the transitional state — the swarm is starting to sense that specialization might be needed.

**When Hybrid is right:**
- The problem is becoming clearer but isn't fully mapped
- One or two specific areas need deeper investigation
- The human senses something but isn't sure what yet
- Testing whether Squadron Mode is warranted before committing

**Biological basis:** Honeybee scout system — most workers continue normal operations while a small number of scouts investigate new food sources or nest sites. The colony doesn't fully commit until scouts report back. [Robinson et al., 2009]

**AI behavior in Hybrid:** Most agents remain in Chorus, but the AI deploys 1-2 specialist lenses (e.g., "I'm going to run a financial analysis on this while the rest of us keep scanning"). The AI might say: "I've deployed an Aegis scout on the security angle — want me to bring in more specialists or stay mostly unified?"

---

### Level 3: SQUADRON (Specialized Swarm)

**The ant colony at peace. Every agent in its role. Maximum efficiency.**

In Squadron Mode, all six squadrons are active with distinct roles, distinct personas, and distinct areas of expertise. Each squadron operates semi-independently, communicating through defined protocols. This is full specialization — the swarm has mapped the problem and each part of it has a dedicated team.

**When Squadron is right:**
- Complex multi-dimensional problems needing simultaneous expertise
- Stable operations where each area needs deep focus
- Adversarial review (Phase 4) — specialized critics from different angles
- Implementation — designers, developers, testers, each in their lane
- Late phases of an initiative where execution needs precision

**Biological basis:** Ant colony with mature division of labor — foragers, nurses, soldiers, builders each optimized for their task. Switching costs are real (30-60% idle time during transitions), so you don't go to Squadron lightly. [Jeanson & Lachaud, 2015]

**AI behavior in Squadron:** Each agent has a distinct system prompt, persona, and evaluation criteria matching its squadron role (Vanguard, Aegis Wing, Sappers, Outriders, Forge, Signal Corps). The AI might say: "We're in full Squadron — Aegis just flagged a security concern, Sappers have a workaround, and Outriders found a precedent. Want me to convene a Chorus moment to synthesize, or let the squadrons keep working?"

## Mode Transition Protocol

### Escalation (Chorus → Hybrid → Squadron)
The AI suggests, the human approves. The AI should proactively recommend escalation when it detects:
- The problem has multiple independent dimensions
- Different parts of the analysis are pulling in different directions
- Specialist knowledge would significantly improve a specific area
- The initiative has moved from exploration to execution

### De-escalation (Squadron → Hybrid → Chorus)
Equally important. The AI should suggest de-escalation when:
- The squadrons are converging on the same conclusion (specialization no longer needed)
- A crisis or surprise requires unified response
- The initiative is entering a new phase that needs fresh scanning
- The human wants to "zoom out" and see the big picture

### Emergency Reversion
Any agent (or the human) can call "Chorus Alert" — an immediate snap back to unified mode. This is the biological equivalent of the colony detecting a predator. No approval needed for the alert; the human confirms the reversion.

## How This Connects to the 3D PEARL Diamond

### Swarm Toggle View
A dedicated toggle in the 3D model that shows ONLY the swarms — stripping away the diamond layers and showing the swarm formations in their full picture. This lets you see:
- Which initiatives have active swarms
- What mode each swarm is in (Chorus/Hybrid/Squadron)
- How the swarms relate to each other

### Mode Visualization
- **Chorus:** All swarm particles same color, moving in unified wave patterns
- **Hybrid:** Most particles unified, 1-2 clusters in distinct colors breaking away
- **Squadron:** Six distinct color-coded formations, each moving in its own pattern

### Universal Swarm Indicators
Every element in the 3D model that CAN be swarmed should show a small swarm indicator — showing whether a swarm is active on it and what mode it's in.
