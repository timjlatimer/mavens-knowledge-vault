# Bingo Card — The Universal Interaction Architecture

**Last Updated:** 2026-02-15
**Source:** HB1000 Architecture v3 (Part VI) + Tim Latimer (direct instruction)

---

## What It Is

The Bingo Card is the HB1000's **universal interaction pattern**. It is not a feature or a module — it is the fundamental unit of interaction in the entire system. Whenever the system needs input, it presents a Bingo Card. Whenever the user needs to take action, it is expressed as a Bingo Card. It is to the HB1000 what the "page" is to the web or the "app" is to a smartphone.

---

## Standard Properties of Every Bingo Card

| Property | Function |
|:---|:---|
| **Squares** | The items to complete — tasks, aspirations, habits, promises, or any actionable item |
| **Multi (Grace)** | An optional AI agent (Grace) attached to help with the card's domain |
| **Graphics** | Embedded infographics and visualizations |
| **PTK Engine** | Commitment tracking layer — watches for promises within squares |
| **Context** | Where in the HB1000 universe the card lives (life area, project, etc.) |
| **Phase** | Completion lifecycle status |
| **KPIs** | Completion rate, time-to-complete, outstanding items |

## The Bingo Card Universe

The user's entire life is viewed through their collection of Bingo Cards — organized by life area, phase, urgency, or any other dimension. A daily Bingo Card update feeds into the Ticker Tape as part of the morning routine (Maven Anthem).

**Examples of Bingo Cards a Ruby Red might have:**
- Family Finances (squares: pay rent, call insurance, build emergency fund)
- Kids' School (squares: parent-teacher meeting, field trip permission, lunch account)
- Health (squares: dentist appointment, prescription refill, exercise 3x/week)
- Career (squares: update resume, apply for promotion, take online course)
- Black Swan Prep (squares: emergency contacts updated, insurance reviewed, will drafted)

## Why the Bingo Card Is a Strategy, Not Just a UI Pattern

Tim has emphasized that the Bingo Card concept needs to be "widely used throughout the HB1000." This is because:

1. **Cognitive Load Reduction:** The Bingo Card reduces complex life management into discrete, visual, completable squares. For Ruby Red (who has 13-14 IQ points of bandwidth stolen by scarcity), this is not a nice-to-have — it is a cognitive prosthetic.

2. **Universal Applicability:** The same pattern works for personal life, organizational management, community engagement, and system onboarding. The setup of the Ticker Tape is itself a Bingo Card. The Learning Loop could be expressed as a Bingo Card. Everything can be a Bingo Card.

3. **PTK Integration:** Every Bingo Card square is a potential promise. The PTK layer watches for commitment-like language and activates when it detects a promise. This is how PTK (the Trojan Horse) gets into people's lives without them noticing — they are just completing Bingo Card squares, and PTK is quietly tracking the ones that involve commitments to others.

4. **Gamification:** The Bingo Card is inherently gamified. Completing squares, filling rows, finishing cards — these are dopamine-triggering completion patterns that keep users engaged without feeling manipulated.

5. **Grace Attachment:** Each Bingo Card can have a Grace (AI agent) attached to it. "Your Grace for Family Finances" helps with that specific card. This is how the Pearl/Grace matriarchal system scales — one Pearl, many Graces, each attached to a Bingo Card.

## Connection to the Universal HB1000 Vision

Tim has emphasized that the end goal is a **universal HB1000 that anybody can use** — where they start putting their own data in, without SIC-specific data pre-loaded. The Bingo Card is the key to this universality because:
- It is culturally neutral (everyone understands a grid of items to complete)
- It is domain-agnostic (works for finances, health, education, career, anything)
- It is self-populating (users create their own squares based on their own life)
- It is the container for all other features (PTK, Grace, Wisdom Works, etc.)
