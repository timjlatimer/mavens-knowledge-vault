# Accessibility & Inclusion Audit — Five Dimensions

**Last Updated:** 2026-02-15

The Accessibility & Inclusion Audit is a mandatory sub-check within Phase 3 (Adversarial Integrity) of the Learning Loop V13.0. Every deliverable must be evaluated across all five dimensions. No dimension may be skipped.

| Dimension | What It Tests | Ruby Red Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Language Barriers** | Non-English speakers, jargon, reading level | Can Ruby Red understand this if English is her second language? Is the reading level appropriate for someone who did not finish college? |
| **Digital Divide** | No internet, no smartphone, limited tech literacy | What if Ruby Red only has a basic phone? What if she shares a computer at the library? |
| **Physical Accessibility** | Hearing, vision, mobility | Can she use this with one hand while holding a child? Can she read it in a dimly lit kitchen? |
| **Time Poverty** | Minimum viable action for busy/exhausted people | Can she complete this in under 5 minutes between school pickup and making dinner? |
| **Emotional Accessibility** | Shame, anxiety, fear, imposter syndrome | Will this make her feel judged? Will it trigger shame about her financial situation? |

Each dimension must receive a **Pass/Fail** with specific findings. The audit ensures that deliverables are not just technically correct but genuinely accessible to the people they are meant to serve.

This audit was added in the V12.9 amendments and is one of the most important innovations in the protocol because it operationalizes the team's empathy commitment into a measurable, enforceable checklist.
