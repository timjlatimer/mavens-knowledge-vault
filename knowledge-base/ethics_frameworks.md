# Ethics Frameworks — Three Modes of Operation

**Last Updated:** 2026-02-15

The SIC Solve Team operates under three configurable ethics frameworks, selected per-execution in the Learning Loop Protocol:

| Framework | Priority | Description | When to Use |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Purpose with Profit** | Social impact first, financial sustainability second | The default for social impact initiatives. Business exists to serve a purpose; profit is the mechanism that sustains the mission, not the mission itself. | Default for all Ruby Red-centered work |
| **Profit with Purpose** | Financial viability first, social benefit integrated | Business optimization with social awareness. The business must be financially healthy, but social benefit is woven into how it operates. | Business optimization, operational efficiency |
| **Profit with Profit** | Pure financial optimization | Cost reduction, efficiency maximization. No social impact lens. | Rare — used only for pure financial analysis |

**Default:** Purpose with Profit is the default ethics framework for the SIC Solve Team. This reflects the team's core belief that "it's expensive to be poor" and that business should be a force for good.

The ethics framework selection shapes how every dimension of the Learning Loop KPI is scored. Under Purpose with Profit, a deliverable that maximizes profit but ignores Ruby Red's needs would score poorly on North Star Alignment.
