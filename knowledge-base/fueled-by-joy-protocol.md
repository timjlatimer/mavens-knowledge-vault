## 16. THE ORAL TRADITION & ORIGINATION STORY

This section captures the personal philosophy and delivery of the Joy Protocol, as dictated by the user. It is the oral tradition that underpins all the "Fueled by JOY" systems in the Village.

### 16.1 The Core Speech

The protocol is delivered as a closing sentiment in speeches, framed as a personal wish.

> "I wish upon you three things. The first thing I wish upon you is the ability to practice **Gratitude**. The second one is the idea of how to manage your **ego** (Modesty). And the third thing I always mention is **selflessness**... And the reason I wish this upon them though is because if you practice attitude of gratitude, metaphorically it'll spell the letter J for you. It'll give you the letter J. And if you practice managing your ego, it'll give you the letter O. And if you practice selflessness... it'll give you the letter Y... So really what I'm wishing upon you is a whole lot of j o y joy in your life and that's the end of the speech."

### 16.2 The Philosophy Behind Each Virtue

*   **Gratitude (J):** Inspired by the power to change one's life by being grateful for the day, turning negatives into positives. The example of Willie Nelson finding gratitude after his troubles with the IRS is cited.
*   **Modesty (O):** Rooted in the understanding that ego is a destructive force, especially for men, causing conflict and harm. Managing ego is crucial; while it can be a force for good, its unmanaged power leads to evil.
*   **Selflessness (Y):** The belief that one's best work is done by building up other people, without one's own agenda. This includes helping people find the right role for them, even if it's outside the organization, to unlock their "Human Brilliance HB 1000." This is also described as "giving without the expectation of return" or the law of reciprocity.

### 16.4 The Physical Gesture

The protocol is often delivered with a physical gesture: holding up three fingers, then revealing them one by one as J, O, and Y are explained.

### 16.3 Human Brilliance (HB 1000)

A concept linked to selflessness, referring to the unique, innate potential within every person. The goal is to bring out this "wet computer" and help people become the best version of themselves.
