# Move 37 — Unconventional Thinking Framework

**Last Updated:** 2026-02-15

Move 37 is named after AlphaGo's famous move 37 in its match against Lee Sedol — a move that no human would have made, that looked like a mistake, but turned out to be brilliant. In the SIC Solve Team context, Move 37 represents the mandate to explore unconventional, community-powered, and village-based approaches to every problem.

In Learning Loop V13.0, Move 37 is set to **Extreme by default**. This means that every protocol execution must actively explore what becomes possible when:
- The North Star has a community/village around them
- A wisdom giant from an unexpected field weighs in
- The approach only works because of community, not despite the lack of it

Move 37 is the antidote to conventional thinking. The conventional answer is the starting point, not the destination. It forces the team to ask: what would we do if we were not constrained by how things have always been done?

The V12.9 amendment made Move 37 Extreme the default setting, meaning it is always active unless explicitly turned off.
