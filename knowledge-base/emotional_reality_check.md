# Emotional Reality Check — Empathy Verification

**Last Updated:** 2026-02-15

The Emotional Reality Check is a mandatory step in Phase 0 of the Learning Loop V13.0 (added in the V12.9 amendments). Before proceeding with any protocol execution, the team must explicitly evaluate:

1. **Does this deliverable acknowledge the emotional reality of the person it serves?**
2. **Does it address the real human experience?** — shame, frustration, fear, exhaustion, confusion, overwhelm
3. **Would the North Star feel seen and respected by this, or lectured at?**

**Why It Matters for Ruby Red:**
Ruby Red is not just financially stressed — she is emotionally exhausted. She carries shame about her financial situation. She feels fear about the future. She is overwhelmed by systems designed to be impenetrable. Any deliverable that ignores this emotional reality, no matter how technically correct, will fail her.

The Emotional Reality Check ensures that empathy — the team's declared superpower — is not just a slogan but a verified component of every deliverable.

**Practical Application:**
- A financial product that requires Ruby Red to disclose her income in a public setting fails the shame test
- A government form that uses legal jargon fails the overwhelm test
- A budgeting app that sends notifications about missed payments fails the fear test
- A service that requires a 45-minute phone call during business hours fails the exhaustion/time poverty test
