## 1. Site Overview & Purpose

**Destiny Discovered** is a free, confidential, 30-question self-assessment built to help individuals uncover their deepest values, purpose, and the principles that guide their lives. The platform positions itself as a **personal North Star discovery tool** — a structured journey of self-reflection that takes approximately 20–30 minutes to complete online, or longer when approached thoughtfully offline.

The site's tagline and primary headline reads:

> **"Discover Your North Star"**
> *A transformative 30-question journey to uncover your deepest values, purpose, and the principles that guide your life.*

The framework is explicitly described as serving three core outcomes:

- **Discover Your Values** — Identify the core principles that guide your thoughts and actions.
- **Clarify Your Purpose** — Understand what you want your life to stand for and the impact you want to make.
- **Gain Self-Awareness** — Recognize your strengths, limitations, and areas for growth.

The site's copyright footer reads: *© 2026 Destiny Discovered. All rights reserved. A tool for personal growth and self-discovery.*

## 4. How It Works — Three-Step Process

The platform guides users through a three-step journey:

Sign in securely to begin your journey. Your responses are private and confidential.

**Step 2 — Answer 30 Questions**
Explore questions about your values, beliefs, purpose, and vision for the future. Take your time — there are no right or wrong answers.

Review your responses and discover patterns in your thinking. See how your values and beliefs shape your worldview.

## 8. Structured Deep-Dive Modules

Two questions use a **structured multi-part format** rather than simple text or list responses:

**Questions 22–23 (Belief System Module):** A branching module that explores the respondent's spiritual or philosophical worldview in depth — their belief, their reasoning, the advantages and disadvantages of that belief, and the evidence they cite.

**Question 30 (Success Attribution Module):** A percentage-allocation exercise requiring the respondent to distribute 100% across four success factors, forcing a quantified self-assessment of how much agency they believe they have over their own outcomes.

---

## 9. Success Attribution Framework (Question 30)

This is one of the most analytically powerful questions in the framework. Respondents must allocate exactly 100% across:

| Factor | Description |
|---|---|
| **Personal Influence** | What you directly control through your own choices, effort, and character |
| **Influence of Others** | The role of mentors, family, community, and relationships |
| **Economic/Social Influences** | Systemic, structural, and environmental forces |
| **Uncontrollable Influences** | Luck, timing, chance, and forces beyond any human control |

The admin dashboard aggregates these responses to show the **average success attribution** across all respondents — a powerful population-level insight into how people perceive agency, privilege, and systemic barriers.

## 15. Strategic Insights for the SIC HB1000 Solve Team

### Why This Framework Matters for the North Star Initiative

Destiny Discovered is not a personality test or a quiz — it is a **structured self-excavation tool**. The 30 questions are designed to surface the respondent's operating system: the values, beliefs, obligations, and worldview that drive every financial and life decision they make. For the SIC HB1000 Solve Team, this has profound implications.

### The Ruby Red Connection

When we consider our Ruby Red client — the 35–45 year old household CFO navigating financial scarcity, cognitive overload, and systemic barriers — this framework becomes a **dignity tool**. It asks her the same questions asked of any CEO, philosopher, or leader. It does not ask about her income, her debt, or her credit score. It asks: *What do you value? What do you stand for? What obligations do you feel?*

This is the "there but for the grace of God go I" principle in action. The framework treats every respondent as a full human being with a rich inner life, not as a problem to be solved.

### Key Strategic Observations

**1. Question 14 is a diagnostic pivot point.** "Is money the most important thing in the world?" — the answer (Yes or No) reframes the entire financial conversation. For Ruby Red, money is not the goal; it is the obstacle between her and her actual values. This question surfaces that truth.

**2. Questions 15 & 16 unlock agency without capital.** By asking for six ways to change the world *without* money before asking about *with* money, the framework establishes that Ruby Red already has power. This is a critical reframe for someone who has been told her whole life that she lacks resources.

**3. Question 17 connects values to obligation.** "Do you feel obligation to do any of the points in questions 15 and 16?" — this is where the framework moves from reflection to activation. It is the bridge between who she is and what she does next.

**4. Question 29 is the empathy mirror.** "Do you believe you have any obligation to assist those who are truly disadvantaged?" — when Ruby Red answers this question, she is simultaneously answering it about herself. This question has the potential to dissolve shame and replace it with solidarity.

**5. Question 30 is the systemic literacy test.** The success attribution breakdown (Personal Influence / Influence of Others / Economic-Social / Uncontrollable) is a quantified measure of whether the respondent understands that *it is expensive to be poor* — and that this is a structural reality, not a personal failure. The aggregate data from this question across a population tells a powerful story about how people perceive the system they live in.

**6. Questions 20 & 21 (Absolutes) are the North Star core.** Listing six absolutes that govern your thoughts and actions, then explaining how each one operates — this is the deepest layer of the framework. These absolutes are the respondent's personal constitution. For the SIC team, this is the raw material of the Prime North Star.

### The Personal North Star vs. The Prime North Star

The framework serves two distinct but related purposes:

| Purpose | Description |
|---|---|
| **Personal North Star** | Discovering who *you* are — your values, your purpose, your worldview, your obligations, your absolutes |
| **Prime North Star** | The collective intelligence that emerges when many people complete this framework — revealing shared values, common obstacles, and population-level insights about agency, belief, and purpose |

The admin dashboard's aggregation of Q20 (Absolutes), Q22 (Belief), and Q30 (Success Attribution) is the infrastructure for the Prime North Star — a living, evolving picture of what a community collectively stands for and believes.
