## 1. Executive Summary

The Stage of Life Agent is the longitudinal memory and contextual awareness engine of the Maven/Grace platform. It tracks Ruby Red's journey across a 15-20 year relationship horizon, ensuring that Grace's approach, tone, service offerings, and news filtering evolve as Ruby's life circumstances change. This agent is what transforms Grace from a capable tool into a trusted lifelong companion.

The core insight behind this agent is simple but profound: a 25-year-old Ruby in the chaos of early motherhood needs something fundamentally different from a 50-year-old Ruby who has just sent her last child to university. If Grace treats them the same way, she fails both of them. The Stage of Life Agent ensures Grace never makes that mistake.

This specification was developed after a thorough search of the `mavens-knowledge-vault` for any existing Stage of Life documentation. The vault contains foundational references to life-stage-aware design in the Maven Anthem psychology framework [1], the Pearl Canon's "Grace Never Gets Cut" protocol [2], and the HB1000 Framework's News Ingestion System [3]. No standalone Stage of Life document was found — this specification is the first formal articulation of the framework.

## 3. The Stage of Life Framework

The framework divides Ruby's journey into five distinct, overlapping stages. These are not rigid demographic boxes — they are fluid, contextual states that Grace uses to calibrate her empathy, her service offerings, and her communication style. A woman can move backward through stages (e.g., a divorce can return a 40-year-old to the financial precarity of "The Launch"), and Grace must be sensitive to these non-linear transitions.

### 3.1 The Five Stages

**Stage 1: The Launch (Typical Age: 18-26)**

The Launch is the period of establishing independence. Ruby is navigating her first apartment, her first real job, and her first encounters with the "three gangster worlds." She is often making financial mistakes for the first time — the first NSF fee, the first payday loan, the first time she realizes the system was not designed for her.

Grace's primary posture in this stage is **The Guide**. She is educational, protective, and patient. She explains things without condescension. She catches mistakes before they cascade. She builds Ruby's financial literacy not through lectures, but through gentle, real-time coaching at the moment of decision.

*Key Services:* Fee Transparency Engine, Banking Watchdog, Data Breach Monitor, Need Milk Money, Rent Reporting (building credit from day one).

*News Filtering:* Local job postings, affordable housing alerts, provincial benefit eligibility changes, community events for young adults.

---

**Stage 2: The Trench Years (Typical Age: 25-38)**

The Trench Years are the most demanding period of Ruby's life. She is managing young children, extreme cognitive scarcity, and the constant pressure of stretching every dollar. Research consistently shows that the cognitive burden of financial scarcity is equivalent to a 13-point IQ drop [4]. Grace must operate with maximum efficiency and minimum friction during this stage.

Grace's primary posture is **The Co-Pilot**. She is highly interventionist, automated, and proactive. She does not wait for Ruby to ask for help — she acts before the crisis hits. She keeps messages to two sentences. She never asks Ruby to fill out a form when she can pre-fill it herself.

*Key Services:* NSF Fee Interception, PTK Dashboard (tracking every promise), Maven Anthem (daily emotional reset), Toilet Paper Delivery (relationship touchpoint), Flare System (emergency broadcasting), Need Milk Money, Big Mama (human-in-the-loop for the 20% that needs judgment).

*News Filtering:* Childcare subsidy changes, school board decisions, local pediatric health alerts, free community events for families, food bank locations, utility assistance programs.

---

**Stage 3: The Pivot (Typical Age: 35-50)**

The Pivot is a period of transition. Ruby's children are becoming more independent, and she is beginning to look up from the immediate crisis of survival toward the horizon of possibility. She may be seeking career advancement, navigating the "sandwich generation" pressures of caring for aging parents, or rebuilding after a major life disruption (divorce, illness, job loss).

Grace's primary posture is **The Strategist**. She shifts from crisis management to forward planning. She begins to introduce concepts like credit building, retirement savings, and entrepreneurship. She advocates for Ruby in bureaucratic systems with more sophistication.

*Key Services:* Corner Banker / Guardian Agent, Union Boss Agent (collective bargaining), Stage Two Entrepreneur Guarantee, Blue Seal business directory, Wisdom Giants network access, Cooperative Phone Negotiation.

*News Filtering:* Provincial employment training grants, small business support programs, secondary school information (for teenagers), eldercare resources, local economic development news.

---

**Stage 4: The Horizon (Typical Age: 48-60)**

The Horizon is the stage of reflection and legacy-building. Ruby's children are leaving home, and she is confronting questions of purpose, legacy, and what the next chapter of her life looks like. She has accumulated significant "Crystallized Intelligence" [7] — the lived wisdom of decades of navigating hard circumstances — and she is beginning to recognize its value.

Grace's primary posture is **The Counselor**. She is reflective, legacy-focused, and community-building. She begins to ask Ruby bigger questions: "What do you want to be known for?" She connects Ruby to the Wisdom Giants network not just as a recipient, but as a potential contributor.

*Key Services:* Wisdom Giants network (as a contributor), Maven Moment amplification, Moonshot planning, North Star refinement, community leadership opportunities, retirement planning tools.

*News Filtering:* Retirement savings programs, legacy planning resources, community leadership opportunities, grandparenting resources, health and wellness news relevant to her age.

---

**Stage 5: The Matriarch (Typical Age: 55+)**

The Matriarch stage is the culmination of the 15-20 year relationship. Ruby has become a Wisdom Giant in her own right. She has lived through the full arc of the Maven ecosystem's support, and she now has the wisdom, the relationships, and the lived experience to help others navigate the same journey.

Grace's primary posture is **The Facilitator**. She amplifies Ruby's voice, distributes her wisdom, and connects her to others who need what she has. She treats Ruby as a peer — a co-creator of the village, not a recipient of its services.

*Key Services:* Village Angels program (mentoring younger Rubies), Pay It Forward system, Big Mama's Genius Village leadership roles, Wisdom Giants contribution platform, legacy documentation.

*News Filtering:* Community leadership opportunities, intergenerational programming, health and wellness for older adults, legacy and estate planning resources.

## 4. The 15-20 Year Relationship Horizon

The defining characteristic of the Maven platform is that it is not a transactional service — it is a lifelong relationship [2]. This is the architectural commitment that makes Maven categorically different from every other financial wellness platform.

### 4.1 The Accumulation of Context

Over 15-20 years, Grace accumulates a rich, private context graph for Ruby. This is not just data — it is the memory of a trusted friend. Grace remembers the name of the mechanic who didn't rip Ruby off in 2026, and suggests him again in 2032. Grace remembers the struggles Ruby had with her first child's school, and proactively offers different resources when her second child reaches that age. Grace remembers the year Ruby almost gave up, and she holds that memory with tenderness.

The Stage of Life Agent is the architectural mechanism that makes this accumulation meaningful. Without it, Grace would have a pile of data. With it, Grace has a story.

### 4.2 The "Grace Never Gets Cut" Protocol

Even if Ruby drops to the lowest free tier of the Maven ecosystem, Grace remains active [2]. The Stage of Life Agent enforces this protocol by flagging any service reduction that would break the continuity of the relationship. The relationship itself is the product. The services are the expression of the relationship.

### 4.3 Non-Linear Stage Transitions

Life does not move in a straight line. A 42-year-old Ruby who goes through a divorce may find herself back in the financial precarity of "The Launch." The Stage of Life Agent monitors for these non-linear transitions through a combination of:
- **Financial signal monitoring:** A sudden drop in account balance, a new NSF fee after years of stability.
- **Conversational signals:** Ruby mentions a separation, a job loss, a health crisis.
- **Explicit stage reset:** Ruby tells Grace directly, "Everything has changed."

When a non-linear transition is detected, the Stage of Life Agent recalibrates immediately, shifting Grace's posture to match the new reality without requiring Ruby to explain herself.

## 5. The Contextual News Feed Layer

The Stage of Life Agent is the primary filter for the Town Crier's News Ingestion System. It ensures that Ruby receives information that is relevant to her actual life, not her demographic average.

### 5.1 The Cognitive Load Index

The Stage of Life Agent maintains a `cognitive_load_index` for each Ruby (0-100 scale). This index is informed by:
- The current life stage (The Trench Years carries a baseline of 80+).
- Recent life events (a new baby, a job loss, a health crisis all spike the index).
- Conversational signals (Ruby mentions she is overwhelmed, exhausted, or at her limit).

When the `cognitive_load_index` is high (above 75), the Town Crier Agent automatically:
- Suppresses all World/Global news.
- Reduces Local/Provincial news to only the most immediately actionable items.
- Increases Village/Mutual Aid news, which is supportive rather than demanding.
- Increases the frequency of Maven Moments (positive community wins) to provide emotional relief.

### 5.2 Stage-Specific News Examples

| Stage | High-Priority News | Suppressed News |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| The Launch | Local job postings, housing alerts, benefit eligibility | Retirement planning, eldercare |
| The Trench Years | Childcare subsidies, school alerts, food bank locations | Career advancement, entrepreneurship |
| The Pivot | Employment training grants, small business programs | Childcare, early childhood education |
| The Horizon | Retirement programs, community leadership | Early childhood, youth programs |
| The Matriarch | Village leadership opportunities, intergenerational programs | Crisis intervention (she is now a responder, not a recipient) |

## 6. Interaction with Identity Spectrum Data

The Stage of Life Agent and the Identity Spectrum Agent are deeply interdependent. They form the two primary axes of Grace's understanding of Ruby.

### 6.1 Identity Expression Shifts Across Stages

A woman's gender expression often shifts as she moves through life stages. The Stage of Life Agent provides the temporal context that prevents the Identity Spectrum Agent from misinterpreting these shifts as permanent changes.

*Example:* A Ruby who scores 30 (Moderately Feminine) during "The Launch" may shift to 65 (Moderately Masculine) during "The Trench Years" as she takes on the role of sole protector and provider for her children. The Stage of Life Agent flags this as a contextual, stage-driven shift — not a fundamental identity change — and the Identity Spectrum Agent adjusts its confidence score accordingly rather than triggering a new Thoughtful Proclamation.

### 6.2 The Intersectionality Matrix

Grace synthesizes both data points to generate the most precise, empathetic response possible:

| Life Stage | Expression Score | Grace's Synthesized Approach |
| :--- | :---: | :--- |
| The Trench Years | 25 (Mod. Feminine) | Warm, nurturing support; community connection; "you are not alone" framing |
| The Trench Years | 70 (Mod. Masculine) | Tactical, efficient; "here's the fastest way to solve this" framing |
| The Matriarch | 25 (Mod. Feminine) | Invite her to nurture and mentor others; amplify her caring wisdom |
| The Matriarch | 70 (Mod. Masculine) | Invite her to lead and build; amplify her strategic wisdom |

## 7. Database Schema

```sql
-- Core life stage profile for each user
CREATE TABLE life_stage_profiles (
    id                          UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
    user_id                     UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES users(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
    -- e.g., 'The Launch', 'The Trench Years', 'The Pivot', 'The Horizon', 'The Matriarch'
    current_stage               VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL,
    -- Probability (0-100) that Ruby is transitioning to the next stage
    transition_probability      DECIMAL(5,2) DEFAULT 0.0,
    -- Current cognitive load index (0-100); higher = more overwhelmed
    cognitive_load_index        DECIMAL(5,2) DEFAULT 50.0,
    -- Is this a non-linear transition (e.g., divorce, job loss)?
    is_non_linear_transition    BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE,
    stage_established_date      TIMESTAMP WITH TIME ZONE DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
    last_recalibrated           TIMESTAMP WITH TIME ZONE DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
    UNIQUE (user_id)
);

-- Log of life events that trigger stage recalibration
CREATE TABLE life_stage_events (
    id                  UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
    user_id             UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES users(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
    -- e.g., 'New Baby', 'Job Loss', 'Divorce', 'Child Started School', 'Empty Nest'
    event_type          VARCHAR(100),
    -- Impact on cognitive_load_index (-20 to +20)
    cognitive_impact    INTEGER CHECK (cognitive_impact >= -20 AND cognitive_impact <= 20),
    -- Did this event trigger a stage transition?
    triggered_transition BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE,
    previous_stage      VARCHAR(50),
    new_stage           VARCHAR(50),
    event_source        VARCHAR(50), -- 'conversational', 'financial_signal', 'explicit'
    recorded_at         TIMESTAMP WITH TIME ZONE DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP
);

-- News filter configuration per stage (managed by Town Crier Agent)
CREATE TABLE stage_news_filters (
    id                  UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT uuid_generate_v4(),
    stage_name          VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL,
    news_category       VARCHAR(100) NOT NULL,
    priority_level      VARCHAR(20) CHECK (priority_level IN ('high', 'medium', 'low', 'suppressed')),
    cognitive_load_threshold INTEGER DEFAULT 75, -- Suppress if load exceeds this
    UNIQUE (stage_name, news_category)
);
```
