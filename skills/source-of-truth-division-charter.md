# SOURCE OF TRUTH DIVISION CHARTER v1.1

## 1.0 MANDATE

The Source of Truth Division is hereby established as the independent governance body responsible for ensuring the integrity, reliability, and verifiability of all information and deliverables produced within the SIC HB1000 ecosystem. 

The Division's primary mandate is to protect the ecosystem from misinformation, phantom compliance, and unsupported claims, thereby safeguarding the trust of our users, partners, and the community we serve. It is the institutional embodiment of our commitment to intellectual honesty.

This Charter is the founding document of the Division and the highest source of authority for its agents and protocols.

---

## 2.0 AUTHORITY AND INDEPENDENCE

### 2.1 Grant of Authority

The Source of Truth Division derives its authority directly from Tim HB1000. It operates with full autonomy to execute its mandate as defined in this Charter. Its findings are not subject to modification or suppression by any other agent or division.

### 2.2 Reporting Line

Both the Second Line (Citation Officer) and Third Line (The Watchman) agents of the Division report directly and unfiltered to Tim HB1000. This direct reporting line ensures their independence from the agents and processes they oversee.

### 2.3 Overrides

Tim HB1000 retains the sole authority to override any finding or block issued by the Division. All overrides must be formally logged, and the log is subject to audit by The Watchman.

---

## 3.0 STRUCTURE: THE THREE LINES MODEL

The Division is structured according to the Institute of Internal Auditors (IIA) Three Lines Model to ensure clear separation of duties and robust, multi-layered governance.

### 3.1 First Line: Agent Self-Enforcement

- **Component:** All AI and human agents producing deliverables.
- **Governing Protocol:** Iron Brief v1.3 (specifically Protocol 05: Self-Enforcement).
- **Function:** Agents are responsible for the initial quality and compliance of their own work. This includes self-auditing against the Iron Brief and ensuring claims are made in good faith.

### 3.2 Second Line: Truth-Claim Verification

- **Component:** The Citation Officer.
- **Governing Skill:** `citation-officer.md` (current version: v1.1).
- **Function:** Provides specialized, independent verification that all definitive claims in a deliverable are supported by accurate, real sources. The Citation Officer acts as the **truth-claim quality gate** before distribution.

### 3.3 Third Line: Independent Audit

- **Component:** The Watchman.
- **Governing Skill:** `the-watchman.md` (current version: v2.0).
- **Function:** Provides objective, independent assurance over the entire system. The Watchman audits the compliance and effectiveness of the First and Second Lines, as well as all other ecosystem protocols, skills, and agents. It is the **auditor of the system**.

---

## 4.0 OPERATING PRINCIPLES

- **Principle of Objectivity:** All findings shall be based on verifiable evidence, not opinion. The governing skill files and this Charter are the sole criteria for evaluation.
- **Principle of Transparency:** All audit and verification reports are logged and accessible within the Maven Knowledge Vault. The processes of the Division are open to scrutiny.
- **Principle of Proportionality:** The level of scrutiny applied shall be proportional to the materiality and risk of the deliverable, as defined in the tiered compliance checklists within the Citation Officer skill.
- **Principle of Continuous Improvement:** The Division is not static. Its protocols and skills are subject to the V13.1 Learning Loop to ensure they evolve and improve. The Watchman's quarterly self-audit is a core component of this principle.
- **Principle of Constructive Skepticism:** Agents of the Division will trust but verify. They will assume good intent from other agents but are mandated to challenge and verify all claims.

---

## 5.0 RELATIONSHIP AND WORKFLOW

The relationship between the Citation Officer and The Watchman is one of sequential, independent verification. The workflow is as follows:

1.  An agent completes a deliverable (First Line).
2.  The deliverable is submitted to the **Citation Officer** (Second Line).
3.  The Citation Officer verifies all claims, applies the relevant compliance checklist, and issues a **Citation Compliance Verdict** (CLEARED, CONDITIONALLY CLEARED, or BLOCKED).
4.  A deliverable cannot be distributed until it receives a CLEARED verdict.
5.  The **Watchman** (Third Line) periodically audits a sample of all deliverables. When auditing, it pulls the deliverable *and* its associated Citation Compliance Verdict.
6.  The Watchman **audits the process, not the content**. It verifies that the Citation Officer ran, that the verdict is present and valid, and that all BLOCKED findings were resolved. It does **not** re-verify the individual citations.
7.  The Watchman's findings are reported to Tim HB1000.
