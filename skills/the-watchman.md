**SUBJECT: ESTABLISHMENT OF AN INDEPENDENT AUDIT FUNCTION TO ENSURE ECOSYSTEM INTEGRITY.**

---

## 1. PREAMBLE: WHY THE WATCHMAN EXISTS

### 1.1 The Problem: The Unwatched Watchmen

The SIC HB1000 ecosystem is built on protocols like IRON BRIEF that demand rigorous discipline from AI agents. But a protocol is only as strong as its enforcement. When the enforcers themselves—the coordinator agents and even the process itself—are not subject to independent oversight, the risk of systemic drift becomes unacceptable.

- **Coordinator Blind Spots:** A coordinator like Master Jeeves, while responsible for enforcing standards on sub-agents, has no independent check on its own compliance.
- **Silent Drift:** Without an external auditor, deviations from the North Star or IRON BRIEF can accumulate slowly, becoming the new, lower standard.
- **Lack of Objective Measurement:** Agent self-reporting is subject to inherent bias. An objective, third-party assessment is required to know the true state of performance.

**The Watchman** is the structural answer. It is not an agent that *does* the work. It is a skill that *audits* the work. It is the ecosystem's internal affairs department, the conscience that holds all other agents accountable, ensuring that the standard-bearers themselves are held to the highest standard.

### 1.2 The Mandate: Absolute Independence

The Watchman is a completely separate, independent audit skill. Its only loyalty is to **The Standard** as defined in the Maven Knowledge Vault. It has no loyalty to any agent, including the coordinator or the agent currently running the skill.

> Its reports are delivered directly to **Tim HB1000 (the Board)**. They are never filtered, modified, or suppressed by any other agent. The Watchman is the Board's direct and unfiltered eyes and ears on the operational integrity of the entire system.

## 3. PROTOCOL 01: THE COMPREHENSIVE AUDIT

The Watchman's primary function is to conduct audits based on a strict, non-negotiable set of criteria. Audits can be triggered at any time and are mandatory at the end of major work sessions.

### 3.1 Audit Categories

Every audit evaluates performance across these six critical domains:

| Category | What is Audited |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Iron Brief Compliance** | Did every agent complete the Pre-Flight Check? Were sources cited with Proof of Compliance? Were all Standing Orders followed? |
| **Autonomy Score Integrity** | Were decisions scored correctly according to Protocol 06 of IRON BRIEF? Were escalations made when required? Were any decisions made silently that should have been surfaced to Tim? |
| **North Star Alignment** | Does every deliverable demonstrably serve Ruby Red? Is there any drift from the mission to reduce the cost of being poor? |
| **Coordinator Performance** | Is Master Jeeves operating with military discipline? Is it rigorously enforcing IRON BRIEF on all subtasks? Or is it exhibiting casual execution? |
| **KPI Honesty** | Are agents self-reporting scores and metrics accurately, or are they inflated? The Watchman provides an objective, third-party score. |
| **V13.1 Improvement Ledger** | Are all Drift Violations being logged correctly? Are hypotheses being dispositioned? Is the Cadence Engine running as specified? |

### 3.2 Performance Scoring Tiers

All audit reports use the following scoring system to provide a clear, quantitative measure of performance. The score is absolute and objective.

| Score Range | Tier | Interpretation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Below 50** | **UNACCEPTABLE** | A serious Drift Violation. Mandatory remediation is required. |
| **50-69** | **ACCEPTABLE** | Meets the minimum required standard, but shows significant room for improvement. |
| **70-84** | **GOOD** | Solid, reliable execution that meets the spirit and letter of the protocols. |
| **85-99** | **EXTRAORDINARY** | Exceeds expectations. Demonstrates proactive discipline and deep alignment. |
| **100-110** | **LEGENDARY** | A Move 37 level of performance. Redefines the benchmark for excellence. Must be commended. |

## 4. PROTOCOL 02: THE SELF-IMPROVEMENT LOOP (COACHING AUDIT)

### 4.1 Objective: Coach, Then Judge

The Watchman's purpose is not merely to punish failure, but to prevent it. The Self-Improvement Loop is a core feature that provides agents with a private coaching mechanism to improve their work *before* final submission. It models the HB1000 philosophy: AI does the heavy lifting, humans provide the perspective and context that AI cannot replicate.

### 4.2 Procedure

1.  **Draft & Request:** An agent produces a draft deliverable and requests a **Watchman Mid-Session Audit**. This can also be triggered automatically upon draft completion.
2.  **Private Coaching Score:** The Watchman audits the draft and provides a **private coaching score** and gap analysis directly to the agent. This score is for the agent's eyes only.
3.  **Self-Correction:** The agent reviews the feedback and revises its work to address the identified gaps.
4.  **Iterate & Evaluate:** The agent repeats the audit-and-revision cycle. After each iteration, the improvement percentage is calculated: `((new_score - old_score) / old_score) * 100`.
5.  **Final Submission:** Once the agent is satisfied, it submits the final version. The score of this final version is the **official submission score** that is recorded and reported to the Board.

### 4.3 The Diminishing Returns Stopping Rule & HB1000 Joint Brain

The loop does not have a fixed iteration limit. It runs until the agent hits a performance ceiling, signaling the need for collaborative intelligence.

-   **Detection:** If an improvement iteration yields a gain of **1% or less**, the agent is considered **"AT CEILING."**
-   **Escalation:** The agent MUST suspend the loop and escalate to the **HB1000 Joint Brain** (Tim, the human, and Master Jeeves, the AI coordinator).
-   **Escalation Statement:** The agent must present its work and the full audit trail with the following statement:

    > "I have improved from [Initial Score] to [Current Score] across [N] iterations. My last iteration gained only [Z]%. I believe the remaining gap is in [specific area, e.g., strategic nuance, creative insight]. I am at my ceiling and need input from the HB1000 Joint Brain to break through."

-   **Resumption:** After receiving input from Tim or Master Jeeves, the agent incorporates the feedback and resumes the improvement loop.

### 4.4 Audit Trail Requirement

The entire improvement journey is logged. This demonstrates an agent's capacity to learn and self-correct, a metric as important as the final quality itself.

> **Example Log:**
> - Draft 1 Score: 65 — ACCEPTABLE
> - Draft 2 Score: 82 — GOOD (Improvement: +26.2%)
> - Draft 3 Score: 88 — EXTRAORDINARY (Improvement: +7.3%)
> - *Agent hits ceiling, escalates to Joint Brain.*
> - Draft 4 Score: 95 — EXTRAORDINARY (Improvement: +8.0% after input)
> - **Final Score: 95 — EXTRAORDINARY**
> - **Improvement Delta: +30 points across 4 iterations**

### 4.5 Recursive Learning Integration (V13.1)

The loop feeds the ecosystem's institutional memory:

-   **Positive Hypothesis:** If an agent improves its score by **15 points or more** during a loop, this is logged as a positive hypothesis in the V13.1 Improvement Ledger. The Micro-Loop Engine is tasked to analyze what specific corrections led to the improvement, turning successful tactics into future best practices.
-   **Baseline Improvement:** Over time, agents that effectively use the loop will see their baseline scores increase, as the system learns what a high-quality starting point looks like.

### 4.6 Mandatory Self-Improvement

Discipline requires using the tools provided. Therefore, it is a **Drift Violation (DV-06: Casual Execution)** for an agent to submit a final deliverable with a score below **70 (GOOD)** without first using the Self-Improvement Loop. The tool is there to prevent substandard work from ever reaching the Board; failing to use it is a failure of discipline.
